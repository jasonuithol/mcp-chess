# Bishop versus Knight

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Bishop_versus_Knight

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Evaluation](/Evaluation "Evaluation") \* [of Pieces](/Evaluation_of_Pieces "Evaluation of Pieces") \* Bishop versus Knight**

**Bishop versus Knight**,
is an often debated topic of the two pieces of similar relative strengths. Traditional thinking has been that the [bishop](/Bishop "Bishop") is slighly better in open positions while the [knight](/Knight "Knight") is better in closed positions [[1]](#cite_note-1). However, the [bishop pair](/Bishop_Pair "Bishop Pair") versus knight and bishop or even more versus two knights yields an extra advantage as demonstrated in the [winning percentages](/Bishop_versus_Knight#WinningPercantages "Bishop versus Knight") of the C, D, E rows of several material configurations 1 - 6 in [Mark Sturman's](/Mark_Sturman "Mark Sturman") table.

# Endgame Considerations

In a minor piece endgame featuring bishop versus knight a couple of special considerations should be taken into account:

- bishop becomes stronger if there are [pawns](/Pawn "Pawn") on both wings
- the side with a knight can defend by the means of blockade on the squares that are out of reach for the enemy bishop
- if the side with a knight has a compact pawn mass, then it should place pawns on the squares controlled by enemy bishop to restrict its mobility.
