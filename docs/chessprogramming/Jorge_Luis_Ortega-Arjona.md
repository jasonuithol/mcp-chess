**Jorge Luis Ortega-Arjona**,  
a Mexican electrical engineer, computer scientist and professor at department of mathematics, [National Autonomous University of Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Autonomous_University_of_Mexico) (UNAM).
He holds a degree in [electrical engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_engineering) in 1992, a M.Sc. in CS in 1996, both from UNAM, and a Ph.D. in CS from [University College London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_College_London), [University of London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_London) in 2007 [[2]](#cite_note-2).
His research interests include [software design pattern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_design_pattern) and [parallel computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallel_computing).
Along with [Manuel Cristóbal López-Michelone](/Manuel_Crist%C3%B3bal_L%C3%B3pez-Michelone "Manuel Cristóbal López-Michelone"), Jorge Luis Ortega-Arjona introduced a pattern description language for [chess positions](/Chess_Position "Chess Position"), published in [ICGA Journal, Vol. 42, No. 1](/ICGA_Journal#42_1 "ICGA Journal") [[3]](#cite_note-3):
