# Protected Passed Pawn

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Protected_Passed_Pawn

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Evaluation](/Evaluation "Evaluation") \* [Pawn Structure](/Pawn_Structure "Pawn Structure") \* [Passed Pawn](/Passed_Pawn "Passed Pawn") \* Protected Passed Pawn**

**Protected Passed Pawn**,  
a passed pawn that is protected by a friendly pawn, and thus gaining some extra "long range" bonus in evaluation since it might become a decisive winning advantage in [pawn endgame](/Pawn_Endgame "Pawn Endgame") assuming other things being equal [[1]](#cite_note-1). In pawn endings, a protected passed pawn typically obligates the opponent king inside its [square of the pawn](/Rule_of_the_Square#TheSquareofthePawn "Rule of the Square"), which makes the defender taboo to capture by the king, freeing the own king from defensive duties.

If the defending pawn is even a passed pawn by itself, the passers are [connected](/Connected_Passed_Pawns "Connected Passed Pawns") [[2]](#cite_note-2). The defender may advance at times to establish a connected [duo](/Duo_Trio_Quart_(Bitboards) "Duo Trio Quart (Bitboards)"), mutually protecting their [stop squares](/Stop_Square "Stop Square") with the option to advance the one or the other passer for a protected passed pawn one rank further. As pointed out by [Robert Hyatt](/Robert_Hyatt "Robert Hyatt") [[3]](#cite_note-3), one should be careful in evaluating connected passers versus two distant isolated passers.
