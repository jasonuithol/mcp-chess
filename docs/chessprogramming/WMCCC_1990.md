The **Tenth World Microcomputer Chess Championship** took place from November 24 to 30, [1990](/Timeline#1990 "Timeline"), in the [Palais des congrès de Lyon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palais_des_congr%C3%A8s_de_Lyon), [Lyon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyon), [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France), at the same time and the same place as the second leg of its human equivalent, the [Kasparov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garry_Kasparov) - [Karpov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatoly_Karpov) [match](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Chess_Championship_1990) [[2]](#cite_note-2) .

# Draw by Reputation

In round five, the mighty [Mephisto](/Mephisto_Lyon "Mephisto Lyon") by [Richard Lang](/Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") played [Échec 1.9](/%C3%89chec "Échec") by [Marc-François Baudot](/Marc-Fran%C3%A7ois_Baudot "Marc-François Baudot") and [Jean-Christophe Weill](/Jean-Christophe_Weill "Jean-Christophe Weill"), and around move 23 the Mephisto team were clearly getting desperate and beginning to envisage the horrifying prospect of losing a lot of prestige - and maybe even the world champion title. Just a few moves later, with Mephisto the exchange down and showing an evaluation of -2, Échec began to repeat moves, and a [draw by repetition](/Repetitions "Repetitions") was fixed on move 32. The explanation was the amateurs had set their [contempt factor](/Contempt_Factor "Contempt Factor") to no less than four Pawns. As [Mike Valvo](/Michael_Valvo "Michael Valvo") remarked in the bulletin: this particular draw by repetition might better be labeled a draw by reputation [[6]](#cite_note-6).
