**Peter Norvig**,  
an American computer scientist, [AI](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") researcher, software developer and director of research at [Google Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google) [[2]](#cite_note-2).
He received a Ph.D. in CS from [University of California, Berkeley](/University_of_California,_Berkeley "University of California, Berkeley") in 1986 on [natural language processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_language_processing) [[3]](#cite_note-3),
has been an assistant professor at the [University of Southern California](/University_of_Southern_California "University of Southern California") and a research faculty member at Berkeley, and was further affiliated with [Stanford University](/Stanford_University "Stanford University"), [Sun Microsystems](/index.php?title=Sun_Microsystems&action=edit&redlink=1 "Sun Microsystems (page does not exist)"), [Harlequin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harlequin_%28software_company%29), [Junglee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Junglee.com) and the [NASA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASA) [[4]](#cite_note-4) , where his division developed the remote agent experiment that flew on the [Deep Space 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_Space_1) spacecraft. He is a fellow of the [AAAI](/AAAI "AAAI"), [ACM](/ACM "ACM"), [California Academy of Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_Academy_of_Sciences) and [American Academy of Arts and Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences) [[5]](#cite_note-5).

# Strachey's Checkers Program

In 2011, Peter Norvig re-published a corrected annotated version of [Christopher Strachey's](/Christopher_Strachey "Christopher Strachey") [Checkers](/Checkers "Checkers") program in [Python](/Python "Python") [[6]](#cite_note-6) [[7]](#cite_note-7), which was originally written in [CPL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CPL_%28programming_language%29), as published 1966 in [Scientific American](/Scientific_American "Scientific American") [[8]](#cite_note-8).

# Online Education

In 2011, along with [Sebastian Thrun](/Sebastian_Thrun "Sebastian Thrun"), Peter Norvig co-teached an online [AI class](/Artificial_Intelligence#UdacityAICourse "Artificial Intelligence") initially through [Stanford University](/Stanford_University "Stanford University"), for which 160,000 students registered and 23,000 completed [[9]](#cite_note-9).
As an outgrowth of free computer science classes, Thrun founded the for-profit educational organization [Udacity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Udacity) [[10]](#cite_note-10).
