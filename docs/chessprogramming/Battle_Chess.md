# Battle Chess

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Battle_Chess

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Engines](/Engines "Engines") \* Battle Chess**

[![](/images/thumb/0/01/Battle_Chess.png/300px-Battle_Chess.png)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Battle_Chess.png)

Cover art of Battle Chess [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Battle Chess**,  
a mass market chess program famous for its [animated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_animation) [move](/Moves "Moves") and specially [capture](/Captures "Captures") sequences in [3D mode](/3D_Graphics_Board "3D Graphics Board"), released for various [home](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_computer) and [personal computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_computer) platforms as well as [video game consoles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_game_console), beside others for [Apple II](/Apple_II "Apple II"), [Commodore 64](/Commodore_64 "Commodore 64"), [Commodore Amiga](/Amiga "Amiga"), [Atari ST](/Atari_ST "Atari ST"), [IBM PC](/IBM_PC "IBM PC"), [Apple Macintosh](/Macintosh "Macintosh"), [Acorn Archimedes](/Acorn_Archimedes "Acorn Archimedes"), [3DO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3DO_Interactive_Multiplayer) and [Nintendo Entertainment System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System).
The game was developed by a team around [Brian Fargo](/index.php?title=Brian_Fargo&action=edit&redlink=1 "Brian Fargo (page does not exist)") and [Michael Quarles](/index.php?title=Michael_Quarles&action=edit&redlink=1 "Michael Quarles (page does not exist)"), including [Jay Patel](/Jay_Patel "Jay Patel"), [Bruce Schlickbernd](/index.php?title=Bruce_Schlickbernd&action=edit&redlink=1 "Bruce Schlickbernd (page does not exist)"), and [Todd Camasta](/index.php?title=Todd_Camasta&action=edit&redlink=1 "Todd Camasta (page does not exist)") [[2]](#cite_note-2), released in 1988 by Fargo's company [Interplay](/index.php?title=Interplay&action=edit&redlink=1 "Interplay (page does not exist)") soon after ending the relationship with [Electronic Arts](/index.php?title=Electronic_Arts&action=edit&redlink=1 "Electronic Arts (page does not exist)") [[3]](#cite_note-3).

# Remake

A remake dubbed **Battle Chess: Game of Kings** was released on [Steam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_(software)) on March 16, 2014 [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5). The program was criticized due to bugs and its playing skills hardly at the level of its predecessors [[6]](#cite_note-6).
