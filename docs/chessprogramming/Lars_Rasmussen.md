# Lars Rasmussen

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Lars_Rasmussen

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* Lars Rasmussen**

**Lars Rasmussen**, [[1]](#cite_note-1)  
a Danish chess player and programmer, endgame and early [endgame tablebase](/Endgame_Tablebases "Endgame Tablebases") researcher
with interests in maximals and [mutual zugzwang](/Zugzwang#MutualZugzwang "Zugzwang") [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3).
Along with [David Forthoffer](/David_Forthoffer "David Forthoffer") and primary author [Sito Dekker](/Sito_Dekker "Sito Dekker"), he contributed to the automated database generation program [Retro](/index.php?title=Retro&action=edit&redlink=1 "Retro (page does not exist)"), considering both [en passant](/En_passant "En passant") and [castling](/Castling "Castling") [[4]](#cite_note-4),
which revealed five discrepancies in KRKB [[5]](#cite_note-5)
from the numbers published by [Jaap van den Herik](/Jaap_van_den_Herik "Jaap van den Herik") and [Bob Herschberg](/Bob_Herschberg "Bob Herschberg"), et al. [[6]](#cite_note-6).
Lars Rasmussen wrote an [MS-DOS](/MS-DOS "MS-DOS") routine for [John Roycroft](/John_Roycroft "John Roycroft") to search text for the extended [GBR code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GBR_code), which, when located, was thoroughly tested for self-consistency [[7]](#cite_note-7).
He further developed the [ChessBase](/ChessBase_(Database) "ChessBase (Database)") interface program to access the [Thompson's Databases](/Thompson%27s_Databases "Thompson's Databases") [[8]](#cite_note-8) [[9]](#cite_note-9), and is author of an engine solving [pawn endgames](/Pawn_Endgame "Pawn Endgame") [[10]](#cite_note-10) [[11]](#cite_note-11) [[12]](#cite_note-12).
