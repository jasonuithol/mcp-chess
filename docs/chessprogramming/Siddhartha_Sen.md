Siddhartha Sen[[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Siddhartha Sen**,  
an American electrical engineer, computer scientist and principal researcher in the [Microsoft](/Microsoft "Microsoft") Research New York City lab, where he leads the AI/Systems group [[2]](#cite_note-2)-
He received his [Master of Engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_of_Engineering) in computer science from [MIT](/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") in 2004, where his thesis advisor was [Charles Leiserson](/Charles_Leiserson "Charles Leiserson") [[3]](#cite_note-3),
and his Ph.D. in computer science at [Princeton University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Princeton_University),
under [Robert Tarjan](/Mathematician#RETarjan "Mathematician") and [Michael Freedman](/Mathematician#MJFreedman "Mathematician") [[4]](#cite_note-4).
His research focuses around [algorithms](/Algorithms "Algorithms") and [data structures](/Data "Data") including [concurrency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concurrency_(computer_science)) in particular for [NUMA](/NUMA "NUMA") architectures.
Along with [Reid McIlroy-Young](/Reid_McIlroy-Young "Reid McIlroy-Young"), [Russell Wang](/Russell_Wang "Russell Wang"), [Jon Kleinberg](/Jon_Kleinberg "Jon Kleinberg") and [Ashton Anderson](/Ashton_Anderson "Ashton Anderson"), Siddhartha Sen is involved in the [Maia Chess](/Maia_Chess "Maia Chess") project of a human-like [neural network](/Neural_Networks "Neural Networks") chess engine [[5]](#cite_note-5).
