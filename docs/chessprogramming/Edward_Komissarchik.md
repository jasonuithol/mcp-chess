**Edward A. Komissarchik**,  
a Russian born mathematician and computer scientist. He graduated from [Moscow State University](/Moscow_State_University "Moscow State University") as a math professor and was researcher at the Institute for System Studies (VNIISI) in [Moscow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moscow), who studied (chess) databases. Along with [Aaron L. Futer](/Aaron_L._Futer "Aaron L. Futer") he analyzed the endgame KQPKQ with white pawn on g7 (b7). Based on [Zermelo's](/Ernst_Zermelo "Ernst Zermelo") justification [[1]](#cite_note-1) they applied a [retrograde analysis](/Retrograde_Analysis "Retrograde Analysis") algorithm, using [bitboards](/Bitboards "Bitboards") in coding the chess board.

Edward Komissarchik and his daughter Julia immigrated from Russia to the United States in 1990. The Komissarchiks, who took on [Palo Alto, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California) built a jobs-search site to showcase their [deep web-search](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_Web) technology and founded *Glenbrook Networks* [[2]](#cite_note-2) in 1999 [[3]](#cite_note-3).
