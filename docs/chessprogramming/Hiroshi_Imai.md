Hiroshi Imai [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Hiroshi Imai**,  
a Japanese mathematician, computer scientist and professor at Graduate School of Information Science and Technology, [University of Tokyo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Tokyo), and head of the *Imai Laboratory*. His research interests include [computational geometry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_geometry), [discrete mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrete_mathematics), [graph theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graph_theory), [computational complexity theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_complexity_theory), [quantum computation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum_computer), [cryptography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryptography) and [tree search](/Search "Search").
Along with [Ayumu Nagai](/Ayumu_Nagai "Ayumu Nagai"), he worked on [depth-first](/Depth-First "Depth-First") [Proof-number search](/Proof-Number_Search "Proof-Number Search") applied to [Shogi](/Shogi "Shogi") and [Othello](/Othello "Othello").
