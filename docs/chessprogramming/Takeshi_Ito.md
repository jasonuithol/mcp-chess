Takeshi Ito [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Takeshi Ito**,  
a Japanese doctor of engineering and assistant professor at Department of Communication Engineering and Informatics, Faculty of Informatics and Engineering, [University of Electro-Communications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Electro-Communications), [Tokyo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dfu,_Tokyo). He serves as chair of the *Cognitive Science and Entertainment (E&C) Research Station* [[2]](#cite_note-2), and as director of the *Computer Shogi Association (CSA)* [[3]](#cite_note-3) [[4]](#cite_note-4). Takeshi Ito is engaged in research on players' thought and [cognitive processes](/Cognition "Cognition") such as [learning](/Learning "Learning"), especially in the context of [Go](/Go "Go") and [Shogi](/Shogi "Shogi"). He is co-author of the Shogi programs [HIT+SS](/index.php?title=HIT%2BSS&action=edit&redlink=1 "HIT+SS (page does not exist)"), a knowledge based system applying a *Selfish Search* [[5]](#cite_note-5), and [Bonanza](/Bonanza "Bonanza") [[6]](#cite_note-6), and a [Ms. Pac-Man](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ms._Pac-Man) bot applying [Monte-Carlo Tree Search](/Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search"), which won the *Screen Capture Competition at CIG 2011* [[7]](#cite_note-7) [[8]](#cite_note-8).
