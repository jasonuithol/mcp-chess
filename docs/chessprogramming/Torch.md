**Torch**,  
A chess engine announced by [Chess.com](/index.php?title=Chess.com&action=edit&redlink=1 "Chess.com (page does not exist)") in 2023, intended as own development for their online chess platform, which played before as Mystery Engine at [CCCC](/index.php?title=CCCC&action=edit&redlink=1 "CCCC (page does not exist)"). With a team consisting of developers and advisors from Chess.com, [OpenBench](/OpenBench "OpenBench") and [Komodo](/Komodo "Komodo").

The team behind Torch is composed of [Andrew Grant](/Andrew_Grant "Andrew Grant") (author of [Ethereal](/Ethereal "Ethereal")), [Finn Eggers](/Finn_Eggers "Finn Eggers") & [Kim Kåhre](/Kim_K%C3%A5hre "Kim Kåhre") (authors of [Koivisto](/Koivisto "Koivisto")), [Jay Honnold](/Jay_Honnold "Jay Honnold") (author of [Berserk](/Berserk "Berserk")), and Michael Whiteley & Dietrich Kappe (current authors of Dragon). The former authors of Dragon, Mark Lefler and Larry Kaufman, are advisors on this project. The development of Torch is supported by many open-source tools, including Pytorch-NNUE, Cutechess, and OpenBench. Torch developers have stated that the entire source code of Torch is original, with no code being used from any other engine.

Torch is a close source without public release as a standalone chess engine. Since October 18, 2023, it has been available on the chess.com platform in analysis mode and offers a faster lightweight and full-feature neural network. On March 20, 2024, Tourch 2 was released unofficially [[1]](#cite_note-1). On June 19, 2024, [Andrew Grant](/Andrew_Grant "Andrew Grant") announced the focus of future Torch development as Chess.com power tool instead of Elo strength[[2]](#cite_note-2).
