**Chris Whittington**,  
a British computer chess and games programmer, publisher and entrepreneur, author of several commercial computer chess programs including [Chess System Tal](/Chess_System_Tal "Chess System Tal") published through his own company [Oxford Softworks](/Oxford_Softworks "Oxford Softworks"). He is famous for his computer chess paradigms as an appeal for knowledge based programs [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3). In May 2000, when Oxford Softworks was sold to a venture capitalist consortium, Chris retired from games programming.

# Rybka Controversy

Chris Whittington was and is critic of the arguments that [Rybka](/Rybka "Rybka") is a derivate of [Fruit](/Fruit "Fruit") since [Zach Wegner](/Zach_Wegner "Zach Wegner") published his first conclusion on the Rybka/Fruit: evaluation in June 2010 [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5), and consequently critic of the [ICGA](/ICGA "ICGA") [decision](/Rybka_Controversy#RybkaDecision "Rybka Controversy") in 2011. During the [Investigation process](/ICGA_Investigations "ICGA Investigations"), he was rejected to become member of the [panel](/ICGA_Investigations#Panel "ICGA Investigations") [[6]](#cite_note-6).
