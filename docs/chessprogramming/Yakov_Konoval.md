**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* Yakov Konoval**

**Yakov Konoval**,  
a Russian chess player and programmer from [Orenburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orenburg), born 1955. From 1968 until 1974, Yakov Konova studied at [Botvinnik's](/Mikhail_Botvinnik "Mikhail Botvinnik") chess school [[1]](#cite_note-1). In 1982 Yakov became a programmer and soon started to combine chess and programming. In 1986-1987 he wrote a program for solving chess problems - one of the fastest for that period, and later worked on [Retrograde Analysis](/Retrograde_Analysis "Retrograde Analysis") for [Endgame Tablebases](/Endgame_Tablebases "Endgame Tablebases").

# 7-man EGTBs

In 2005 Yakov Konoval started to collaborate with [Marc Bourzutschky](/Marc_Bourzutschky "Marc Bourzutschky") on constructing 7-man EGTBs. Yakov wrote the generator and Marc a verification program and some utilities for extracting the data. Marc also does the generation of 7-man EGTB on his home computers.

Quote by [Guy Haworth](/Guy_Haworth "Guy Haworth") from [CCRL](/CCRL "CCRL") [Discussion Board Endgame Tablebases](/Computer_Chess_Forums "Computer Chess Forums"), May 17, 2007 [[2]](#cite_note-2).
Yakov Konoval has a new super-fast code that computes 7-man EGTs to the DTC(onversion) metric. Marc Bourzutschky has production-run this code to create several P-less endgames' EGTs and a few P-ful ones too.
I think that further development of the code, to create P-ful endgames' EGT to the DTZ metric, P-slice by P-slice, is on the back-burner at the moment, but maybe there's some more production-running going on.

# EGTB records

In October 2005, Yakov Konoval and [Marc Bourzutschky](/Marc_Bourzutschky "Marc Bourzutschky") announced that a position in the ending of a KRRNkrr requires 290 moves to convert to a simpler winning endgame [[3]](#cite_note-3). The old record was 243 moves from a position in a rook and knight versus two knights endgame, discovered by [Lewis Stiller](/Lewis_Stiller "Lewis Stiller") in 1991 [[4]](#cite_note-4).

In March 2006 the wizards of 7-men endgames, [Marc Bourzutschky](/Marc_Bourzutschky "Marc Bourzutschky") and Yakov Konoval found a 330 moves win in KQBNkqb [[5]](#cite_note-5) and in May 2006 a 517 moves win in KQNkrbn [[6]](#cite_note-6) [[7]](#cite_note-7).
