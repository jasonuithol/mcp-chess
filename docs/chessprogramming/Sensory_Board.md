# Sensory Board

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Sensory_Board

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [User Interface](/User_Interface "User Interface") \* Sensory Board**

[![](/images/thumb/0/05/CornellBoard1_small.jpg/300px-CornellBoard1_small.jpg)](http://people.ece.cornell.edu/land/courses/ece4760/FinalProjects/s2008/eaj24_wpb3/eaj24_wpb3/index.html)

Glass Sensory Board [[1]](#cite_note-1)

A **Sensory Board** is a [chessboard](/Chessboard "Chessboard") with [switches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Switch) or [sensors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sensor) to recognize [moves](/Moves "Moves") or [pieces](/Pieces "Pieces"), which acts as dedicated input device to [enter moves](/Entering_Moves "Entering Moves") (or [positions](/Chess_Position "Chess Position")) for playing or analyzing [chess games](/Chess_Game "Chess Game") along with [chess playing](/Engines "Engines") and [recording](/Databases "Databases") software, also integrated in [dedicated chess computers](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers"). Rank-File or Square [LEDs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light-emitting_diode) may indicate a move internally made by a chess program.
