**Xenarbor**,  
a chess program written in [Fortran](/Fortran "Fortran") [[2]](#cite_note-2) by [Donald Miller](/index.php?title=Donald_Miller&action=edit&redlink=1 "Donald Miller (page does not exist)") running on [IBM 370](/IBM_370 "IBM 370") [[3]](#cite_note-3) and [IBM 3081](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IBM_3081) (1984) mainframes [[4]](#cite_note-4), playing the [ACM 1974](/ACM_1974 "ACM 1974"), [ACM 1976](/ACM_1976 "ACM 1976"), [ACM 1977](/ACM_1977 "ACM 1977") (2½/4) and [ACM 1984](/ACM_1984 "ACM 1984"). Xenarbor was further involved in promoting [MyChess](/MyChess "MyChess") by [David Kittinger](/David_Kittinger "David Kittinger"), which running on a [Cromemco Z-2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cromemco_Z-2) with [Z80](/Z80 "Z80") processor won a match from Xenarbor 4 at an event of the [Santa Clara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Clara,_California) Chess Club in 1979 [[5]](#cite_note-5).

# Etymology

The program's name combines [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language) [[6]](#cite_note-6) and [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin) [[7]](#cite_note-7) roots to mean "strange or alien tree", as explained by Miller to [Boris Baczynskyj](/Boris_Baczynskyj "Boris Baczynskyj") during [ACM 1984](/ACM_1984 "ACM 1984") [[8]](#cite_note-8).
