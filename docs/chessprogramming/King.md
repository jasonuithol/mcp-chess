[Max Ernst](/Category:Max_Ernst "Category:Max Ernst") - The King Playing with the Queen [[1]](#cite_note-1)

The **King** is the most important piece in chess, since it is the object of the game to [checkmate](/Checkmate "Checkmate") the opponent's king. Each side has exactly one king. Despite his importance, the king is not the most powerfull piece and can only [move](/Moves "Moves") one step in all eight [directions](/Direction "Direction") if the [target square](/Target_Square "Target Square") is not occupied by an own piece and not controlled by any opponent piece. A special kind of move involving king and [rook](/Rook "Rook") from their [initial position](/Initial_Position "Initial Position") and may occur only once in a game is [castling](/Castling "Castling"). The closest [distance](/Distance "Distance") between both kings origins along a [rank](/Ranks "Ranks") or [file](/Files "Files") is the so called [opposition](/Opposition "Opposition") with a [Manhattan-distance](/Manhattan-Distance "Manhattan-Distance") of two.
