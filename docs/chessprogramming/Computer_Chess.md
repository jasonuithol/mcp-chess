**Computer Chess**, (Atari Chess, CXL-4009)  
a [cartridge](/Module "Module") chess program for [Atari 8-bit](/Atari_8-bit "Atari 8-bit") home computers by [Larry Wagner](/Larry_Wagner "Larry Wagner") and [Bob Whitehead](/index.php?title=Bob_Whitehead&action=edit&redlink=1 "Bob Whitehead (page does not exist)") (Graphics) in [6502](/6502 "6502") [Assembly](/Assembly "Assembly"), the game AI with the help of [Julio Kaplan](/Julio_Kaplan "Julio Kaplan") [[2]](#cite_note-2), initially the development version or prototype of [Video Chess](/Video_Chess "Video Chess"), released for the Atari 2600 in 1978 [[3]](#cite_note-3).
In 1979, a revised version of the program was released by [Atari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atari) as *Computer Chess*, adapted for the Atari 400/800.
A [joystick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joystick) was used to move the pieces.

# Tournament Play

Two instances of the program played the [North American Microcomputer Chess Championship](/MCC_1980 "MCC 1980") 1980 in [San Jose, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Jose,_California), where Atari A gained a respectable 50% score [[4]](#cite_note-4).
