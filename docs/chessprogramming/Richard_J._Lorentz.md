**Richard Joseph Lorentz**,  
an American computer scientist and professor of Computer Science at [California State University, Northridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California_State_University,_Northridge). His major research activity involves the study of game playing programming. He is co-author and supervisor of the successful [Amazons](/Amazons "Amazons") program [Invader](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=249), co-author of the [Havannah](/Havannah "Havannah") program [Wanderer](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=605), and the [Dots and Boxes](/index.php?title=Dots_and_Boxes&action=edit&redlink=1 "Dots and Boxes (page does not exist)") program [Matadots](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=565). His main interests is to expand [Monte-Carlo tree search](/Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search") algorithms that exist in Invader and Wanderer [[2]](#cite_note-2) .
