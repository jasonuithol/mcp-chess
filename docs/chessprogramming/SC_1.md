SC 1 [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Schachcomputer SC 1**,  
[VEB Funkwerk Erfurt's](/VEB_Mikroelektronik_Erfurt "VEB Mikroelektronik Erfurt") first [dedicated chess computer](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") species from 1981, build in low quantities of only about 12 to 20 pieces. The SC 1 was merely a prototype kind of a chess computer, to test the performance of their own [Z80](/Z80 "Z80") clone, the [U880](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U880) 8-bit processor [[2]](#cite_note-2) . The program wasn't yet an own development [[3]](#cite_note-3), but a program from the "[Enemy of the people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enemy_of_the_people)", a plain copy of [Ron Nelson's](/Ron_Nelson "Ron Nelson") program of a [Chess Challenger](/Chess_Challenger "Chess Challenger") manufactured by [Fidelity Electronics](/Fidelity_Electronics "Fidelity Electronics") [[4]](#cite_note-4). Remarkably, its successor, the [SC 2](/SC_2 "SC 2"), which was produced in higher quantities and exported to the [capitalistic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capitalism) "[First World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_World)" to merit foreign exchanges, still was a slightly modified Ron Nelson program [[5]](#cite_note-5) [[6]](#cite_note-6).
