# Threat Move

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Threat_Move

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Chess](/Chess "Chess") \* [Moves](/Moves "Moves") \* Threat Move**

The **Threat Move** is a quite general term in chess or computer chess, since any forced [tactical](/Tactical_Moves "Tactical Moves") or attacking move might be considered a threat move. Anyway, it seems the term is usually related to [null move pruning](/Null_Move_Pruning "Null Move Pruning"), and refers to a move which refutes a [null move](/Null_Move "Null Move") by a considerable [fail-soft](/Fail-Soft "Fail-Soft") margin. It might be used along the [Killer Heuristic](/Killer_Heuristic "Killer Heuristic") to refute [siblings](/Sibling_Node "Sibling Node") of the child node below the null move. Beside [move ordering](/Move_Ordering "Move Ordering") issues, [threat move detection](/Null_Move_Pruning#ThreatDetection "Null Move Pruning") might enable or trigger [extensions](/Extensions "Extensions"), for instance [mate threat extensions](/Mate_Threat_Extensions "Mate Threat Extensions") or the [Botvinnik-Markoff extension](/Botvinnik-Markoff_Extension "Botvinnik-Markoff Extension").
