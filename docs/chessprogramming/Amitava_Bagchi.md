# Amitava Bagchi

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Amitava_Bagchi

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* Amitava Bagchi**

**Amitava Bagchi**,  
an Indian physicist, computer scientist and professor emeritus at Management Information Systems at the [Indian Institute of Management, Calcutta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Institute_of_Management_Calcutta), and [West Bengal University of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maulana_Abul_Kalam_Azad_University_of_Technology) [[1]](#cite_note-1).
He received his Ph.D. under [Ronald da Silva Meyer](/Mathematician#ARMeyer "Mathematician") at [Massachusetts Institute of Technology](/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") in 1972 on *Topics in Complexity Theory and Recursion Theory*.
His research interests include [software engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_engineering), [software reliability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_quality), [software verification and validation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_verification_and_validation), analysis of [algorithms](/Algorithms "Algorithms"), and [combinatorial auctions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combinatorial_auction).
Along with his Ph.D. students, he worked and published on [search algorithms](/Search "Search"). Amitava Bagchi and [Subir Bhattacharya](/Subir_Bhattacharya "Subir Bhattacharya") investigated [Stockman's](/George_Stockman "George Stockman") [SSS\* algorithm](/SSS*_and_Dual* "SSS* and Dual*"), and proposed a [recursive](/Recursion "Recursion") implementation, called [RecSSS\*](/SSS*_and_Dual*#RecSSS "SSS* and Dual*").
