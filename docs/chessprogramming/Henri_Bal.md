**Henri Elle Bal**,  
a Dutch mathematician, computer scientist and professor at [Vrije Universiteit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit), [Amsterdam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam). He is a influential and luminary researcher and authority in [parallel computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallel_computing), [computer cluster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_cluster), parallel programming languages, optimizing compiler and parallel applications. Bal has been a member of over 30 program committees, and as such has had a major impact on the field of parallel computing [[2]](#cite_note-2) . Henri Bal received a M.Sc. in Mathematics from the [Delft University of Technology](/Delft_University_of_Technology "Delft University of Technology") in 1982, and Ph.D. in CS [[3]](#cite_note-3) in 1989 under the supervision of [Andrew S. Tanenbaum](/Mathematician#Tanenbaum "Mathematician"). He was visiting researcher at [Imperial College, London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_College_London), [University of Arizona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Arizona), [Tucson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tucson) and [Massachusetts Institute of Technology](/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") [[4]](#cite_note-4). Along with [Robbert van Renesse](/Robbert_van_Renesse "Robbert van Renesse") in 1986, he wrote papers on [parallel alpha-beta](/Parallel_Search "Parallel Search"), with [Victor Allis](/Victor_Allis "Victor Allis") in 1995 on [parallel retrograde analysis](/Retrograde_Analysis "Retrograde Analysis") [[5]](#cite_note-5), and in 2002, with his student [John Romein](/John_Romein "John Romein"), he solved the game of [Awari](/Awari "Awari") [[6]](#cite_note-6) [[7]](#cite_note-7) .

# DAS-3

Henri Bal was the driving force behind the acquisition and use of three large distributed cluster computers called the *Distributed ASCI Supercomputer* (DAS), a Computer Science [Grid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grid_computing) with revolutionary [Optical Interconnect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_interconnect) [[8]](#cite_note-8) .
