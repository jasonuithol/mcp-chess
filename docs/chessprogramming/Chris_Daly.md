Chris Daly [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Chris Daly**,  
an American electrical engineer, in the 70s affiliated with the [NASA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NASA) [Goddars Space Flight Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goddard_Space_Flight_Center), [Greenbelt, Maryland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenbelt,_Maryland) [[2]](#cite_note-2) . Along with [Kenneth L. King](/Kenneth_L._King "Kenneth L. King") from [Information Displays, Inc.](http://deletionpedia.dbatley.com/w/index.php?title=Information_Displays_Inc._%28deleted_14_Aug_2008_at_11:02%29#_note-0) (IDI) [[3]](#cite_note-3) [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5), the designer and manufacturer of the stand-alone [computer-aided design](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer-aided_design) (CAD) platform [IDIIOM](/Kenneth_L._King#IDIIOM "Kenneth L. King") (IDI Input-Output Machine) [[6]](#cite_note-6), Chris Daly was primary author of the chess program running on the IDIIOM system utilizing a [Varian 620/i minicomputer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varian_Data_Machines) [[7]](#cite_note-7) [[8]](#cite_note-8) . The program, later dubbed [Daly CP](/Daly_CP "Daly CP"), won second place in the [First United States Computer Chess Championship](/ACM_1970 "ACM 1970") 1970 in [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City) [[9]](#cite_note-9) .
