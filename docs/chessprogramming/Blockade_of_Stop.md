# Blockade of Stop

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Blockade_of_Stop

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Evaluation](/Evaluation "Evaluation") \* [of Pieces](/Evaluation_of_Pieces "Evaluation of Pieces") \* Blockade of Stop**

**Blockade of Stop**,  
the obstruction of the [stop square](/Stop_Square "Stop Square") of a [pawn](/Pawn "Pawn") mechanically prevents its advancement. While two opponent pawns head on head on the same file, a [pawn ram](/Pawn_Rams_(Bitboards) "Pawn Rams (Bitboards)"), mutually block each other, the blockade of stop usually refers to [pieces](/Pieces "Pieces").

```
The more general term Blockade also refers to a chess tactics where a decoy sacrifice is conducted to decisively reduce the mobility of pieces or king as start of a combination.
```

# Passed Pawns

Most notably in [passed pawn](/Passed_Pawn "Passed Pawn") evaluation [[1]](#cite_note-1), the blockade of stop reduces the bonus by up to one rank less advanced or even more. Programs may further scale the blockade of stop penalty depending on piece type and color, where an opponent [bishop](/Bishop "Bishop"), followed by opponent [king](/King "King") and [knight](/Knight "Knight") are most effective to blockade the stop. A [rook](/Rook "Rook"), either an own or opponent one, might already considered by the [Tarrasch rule](/Tarrasch_Rule "Tarrasch Rule"), despite that rule also covers [tele stops](/Pawn_Spans#StopandDistantStop "Pawn Spans").

# Open Pawns

In his *Little Chess Evaluation Compendium*, [Lyudmil Tsvetkov](/Lyudmil_Tsvetkov "Lyudmil Tsvetkov") votes for a bonus for blocking none passed but [open pawns](/Open_Pawns_(Bitboards) "Open Pawns (Bitboards)") without an opponent counter pawn on its [front span](/Pawn_Spans "Pawn Spans") [[2]](#cite_note-2).

# Development

During the [opening](/Opening "Opening"), some programs apply penalties for stop square obstruction by own pieces of none developed center pawns.
