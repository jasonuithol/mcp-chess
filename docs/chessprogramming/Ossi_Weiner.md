Ossi Weiner [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Ossi Weiner**,  
a German graduate engineer, chess player, computer chess expert, co-author of computer chess books, [Opening Book](/Opening_Book "Opening Book") [author](/Category:Opening_Book_Author "Category:Opening Book Author"), and computer chess and games businessman and distributor. Weiner was consultant and product manager of [Hegener & Glaser](/Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser") [[2]](#cite_note-2), representing [Mephisto](/Mephisto "Mephisto") chess computers at various [World Computer](/World_Computer_Chess_Championship "World Computer Chess Championship") and [World Microcomputer Chess Championships](/World_Microcomputer_Chess_Championship "World Microcomputer Chess Championship"), yielding in long term cooperation with [Richard Lang](/Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") and [Ed Schröder](/Ed_Schroder "Ed Schroder"). In 1983, Weiner founded his own company, the *Hobby Computer Centrale, Weiner Vertriebs GmbH* in Munich, which was the forerunner of the [Millennium 2000 GmbH](/Millennium_2000_GmbH "Millennium 2000 GmbH"), founded 1996 together with his partner [Manfred Hegener](/Manfred_Hegener "Manfred Hegener") [[3]](#cite_note-3) . Millennium 2000 further distributed the World Champion chess programs [Shredder](/Shredder "Shredder") by [Stefan Meyer-Kahlen](/Stefan_Meyer-Kahlen "Stefan Meyer-Kahlen"), and after [Marty Hirsch's](/Marty_Hirsch "Marty Hirsch") problems [[4]](#cite_note-4) with his former distributor [Martin Stamer](/Martin_Stamer "Martin Stamer") his program [MChess Pro](/MChess "MChess"). In 2003, Millennium discontinued the high end computer chess software sector to focus on their core business, [chess computers](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers"), electronic games and consumer products. According to [Hein Veldhuis](/Hein_Veldhuis "Hein Veldhuis"), the computers of Millennium 2000 are produced by [Eric White](/Eric_White "Eric White"), while [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy") is responsible for the programs [[5]](#cite_note-5).
