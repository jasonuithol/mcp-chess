# Chess960CWC 2008

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Chess960CWC_2008

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Tournaments](/Tournaments_and_Matches "Tournaments and Matches") \* [Livingston Chess960 Computer World Championship](/Livingston_Chess960_Computer_World_Championship "Livingston Chess960 Computer World Championship") \* Chess960CWC 2008**

**[< Prev](/Chess960CWC_2007 "Chess960CWC 2007") [Next >](/Chess960CWC_2009 "Chess960CWC 2009")**

The **Fourth Livingston** [[1]](#cite_note-1) [Chess960](/Chess960 "Chess960") **Computer World Championship** was held within the scope of the [Chess Classic](http://www.chesstigers.de/ccm9.php?lang=1) [Mainz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainz), Germany, July 30 - August 1, [2008](/Timeline#2008 "Timeline"), organized by [Hans-Walter Schmitt](/index.php?title=Hans-Walter_Schmitt&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hans-Walter Schmitt (page does not exist)") and his Chess Tigers [[2]](#cite_note-2) . The two finalists from [2007](/Chess960CWC_2007 "Chess960CWC 2007"), [Rybka](/Rybka "Rybka") and [Shredder](/Shredder "Shredder") were challenged by two qualifiers from an online tournament on [Internet Chess Club](/index.php?title=Internet_Chess_Club&action=edit&redlink=1 "Internet Chess Club (page does not exist)") (ICC) [[3]](#cite_note-3), [Naum](/Naum "Naum") and [Deep Sjeng](/Deep_Sjeng "Deep Sjeng"). [Hans Secelle](/Hans_Secelle "Hans Secelle") served as arbiter, and [Eric van Reem](/Eric_van_Reem "Eric van Reem") as Tournament director.
