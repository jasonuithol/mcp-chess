The [ACM's](/ACM "ACM") **Third North American Computer Chess Championship** (NACCC) was held from August 13 to 15, [1972](/Timeline#1972 "Timeline") at the Sheraton Boston Hotel [[2]](#cite_note-2) , [Boston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston%2C_Massachusetts), [Massachusetts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts) [[3]](#cite_note-3) .

# Panel Discussion

On the morning of the last day of the tournament the authors of the programs met for a panel discussion [[6]](#cite_note-6). [Professor Mittman](/Ben_Mittman "Ben Mittman") served as moderator. Other members of the panel were [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy"), [Jim Gillogly](/James_Gillogly "James Gillogly"), [Al Zobrist](/Albert_Zobrist "Albert Zobrist"), [George Arnold](/George_Arnold "George Arnold"), [Franklin Ceruti](/Franklin_D._Ceruti "Franklin D. Ceruti"), [David Slate](/David_Slate "David Slate"), [Ed Kozdrowicki](/Ed_Kozdrowicki "Ed Kozdrowicki"), and [Monty Newborn](/Monroe_Newborn "Monroe Newborn"). The panel arrived a few conclusions:

1. a need for including algorithms controlling long-range [strategy](/Strategy "Strategy") was emphasized
2. there was interest in the [USC](/USC_CP "USC CP") approach
3. [self-learning](/Learning "Learning") was confirmed as being a long way off. [Arthur Samuel](/Arthur_Samuel "Arthur Samuel") was in the audience and participated actively in the discussion.
