**Connor McMonigle**,  
an American computer science major at the [University of Washington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Washington) and member of its *Advanced Robotics* team [[2]](#cite_note-2).
He is author of the [UCI](/UCI "UCI") compliant [open source chess engine](/Category:Open_Source "Category:Open Source") [Seer](/Seer "Seer") using a custom [NNUE](/NNUE "NNUE") implementation, first released in October 2020 [[3]](#cite_note-3).
His [GitHub](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GitHub) repository further provides a [C++](/Cpp "Cpp") template based [neural network](/Neural_Networks "Neural Networks") reference implementation [[4]](#cite_note-4),
and the **cartesian-gp-chess-engine**, an experiment involving evolving cartesian [genetic programs](/Genetic_Programming "Genetic Programming") to evaluate chess positions, using self play to evolve slightly superior chess playing cartesian programs each generation [[5]](#cite_note-5).
