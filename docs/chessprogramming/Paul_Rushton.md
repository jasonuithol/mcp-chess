**Paul Gerald Rushton**, (born April 15, 1949)  
a English-born, Canadian mathematician, chemist, computer scientist and chess programmer. He holds undergraduate degrees from [Dalhousie University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dalhousie_University) in mathematics, chemistry and education, and a M.Sc. in computing science from [University of Alberta](/University_of_Alberta "University of Alberta"), with a thesis titled *A Critique of Programming Techniques for Playing Chess* [[1]](#cite_note-1) [[2]](#cite_note-2).
As a graduate student he started to write his first chess-playing program, and continued his research on techniques for chess and game playing programs of that time, and published several papers on that topic along with [Tony Marsland](/Tony_Marsland "Tony Marsland").

# Early Research

Beside the ideas and incomplete programs by [Jack Good](/Jack_Good "Jack Good") [[3]](#cite_note-3) and [Mikhail Botvinnik](/Mikhail_Botvinnik "Mikhail Botvinnik") [[4]](#cite_note-4), following chess programs were investigated by Paul Rushton and [Tony Marsland](/Tony_Marsland "Tony Marsland") as published in 1973 [[5]](#cite_note-5).

- [Chess 3.5](/Chess_(Program) "Chess (Program)") [[6]](#cite_note-6)
- [Coko](/Coko "Coko") [[7]](#cite_note-7)
- [Greenblatt Chess Program](/Mac_Hack "Mac Hack") [[8]](#cite_note-8)
- [NSS](/NSS "NSS") [[9]](#cite_note-9)
- [Tech](/Tech "Tech") [[10]](#cite_note-10)
- [Wita](/Awit "Awit")

# Restart

Paul Rushton went into business for 27 years but returned to teaching computing at [University of the Fraser Valley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_the_Fraser_Valley) in 2000, where he restarted his chess research and the development of a goal-directed knowledge-based chess-playing program that [learns](/Learning "Learning"), which he continues after his retirement in 2010 [[11]](#cite_note-11).
