Matthieu Walraet [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Matthieu Walraet**,  
a French [Go](/Go "Go") player [[2]](#cite_note-2) and software engineer with a masters degree in computer science from [École Nationale Supérieure d'Informatique pour l'Industrie et l'Entreprise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89cole_nationale_sup%C3%A9rieure_d%27informatique_pour_l%27industrie_et_l%27entreprise) in 1996 [[3]](#cite_note-3). He has contributed to various open source projects, such as [Luc Vannier's](/index.php?title=Luc_Vannier&action=edit&redlink=1 "Luc Vannier (page does not exist)") *OpenGotha* Go tournament management program [[4]](#cite_note-4), and the [WireWorld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wireworld) [cellular automaton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellular_automaton) [[5]](#cite_note-5).

# A googolplex of Go games

In *A googolplex of Go games* in 2016, Matthieu Walraet elaborated on the number of possible Go games on a 19x19 board. Inspired by the work of [John Tromp](/John_Tromp "John Tromp") on the number of legal Go positions and John Tromp and [Gunnar Farnebäck](/Gunnar_Farneb%C3%A4ck "Gunnar Farnebäck") on the number of possible Go games on a board less than 19x19, who already established lower and upper bounds of

:   [![Googleplex1.png](/images/b/b9/Googleplex1.png)](/File:Googleplex1.png)

Using properties of binary [Gray code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gray_code), Matthieu Walraet narrowed the huge gap to a lower bound of

:   [![Googleplex2.png](/images/6/63/Googleplex2.png)](/File:Googleplex2.png)

well above a [googolplex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Googolplex) by subdividing the board and iterating over all legal sub-board positions. A revised version of his paper was presented along with John Tromp at the [CG 2016](/CG_2016 "CG 2016") conference [[6]](#cite_note-6).
