# Databases

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Databases

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Software](/Software "Software") \* Chess Databases**

**Chess Databases**,  
an organized collection of [chess games](/Chess_Game "Chess Game") to perform [selects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Select_(SQL)), [updates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Update_(SQL)), [inserts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insert_(SQL)) and [deletes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delete_(SQL)) of games through a [query language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Query_language) or [data manipulation language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_manipulation_language), most often implemented as [dialogue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dialog_box) oriented [user interface](/User_Interface "User Interface") of a [backend](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Back-end_database). Data and query results of chess databases are typically organized as [list](/Linked_List "Linked List") of games, or as tree of [positions](/Chess_Position "Chess Position") which nodes index the games. They usually consist of multiple related [computer files](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_file) keeping [header information](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Header_(computing)), annotations, [moves](/Moves "Moves"), [classifiers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classifier) such as [opening keys](/Opening_Book "Opening Book") à la [ECO](/ECO "ECO") or [NIC-Key](/NIC-Key "NIC-Key"), [hashes](/Zobrist_Hashing "Zobrist Hashing") and [indices](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Database_index) for fast retrieval, quite similar to [ISAM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISAM).
