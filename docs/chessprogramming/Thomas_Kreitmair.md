**Thomas Kreitmair**,  
a German electrical engineer, research scientist affiliated with the [NATO C3 Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NC3O), chess player and former chess programmer. Along with [Matthias Engelbach](/Matthias_Engelbach "Matthias Engelbach"), Thomas Kreitmair is co-author of [Schach 3.0](/Schach "Schach"), the complete re-write of the [Algol](/Algol "Algol") program Schach **2.x** by Engelbach in [x86](/X86 "X86") (486) [assembly language](/Assembly "Assembly") for [PCs](/IBM_PC "IBM PC"). Residing in [The Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands) [[2]](#cite_note-2), Thomas Kreitmair with Schach 3.0 became regular participant at the [Dutch Open Computer Chess Championships](/Dutch_Open_Computer_Chess_Championship "Dutch Open Computer Chess Championship") and [Aegon Tournaments](/Aegon_Tournaments "Aegon Tournaments").
