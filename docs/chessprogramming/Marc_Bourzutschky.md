**Marc Stephen Bourzutschky**,  
an American physicist and researcher in the field of [Retrograde Analysis](/Retrograde_Analysis "Retrograde Analysis") and [Endgame Tablebases](/Endgame_Tablebases "Endgame Tablebases"). He is creator of the six piece EGTBs for [Johan de Koning's](/Johan_de_Koning "Johan de Koning") compact [Chessmaster](/Chessmaster#EGTB "Chessmaster") format. Since 2005, Marc Bourzutschky collaborated with [Yakov Konoval](/Yakov_Konoval "Yakov Konoval") on 7-man EGTB. Yakov wrote the generator and Marc the verification program and some utilities for extracting data [[1]](#cite_note-1) [[2]](#cite_note-2).

# EGTB records

In October 2005, Marc Bourzutschky and Yakov Konoval announced that a position in the ending of a KRRNkrr requires 290 moves to convert to a simpler winning endgame [[3]](#cite_note-3). The old record was 243 moves from a position in a rook and knight versus two knights endgame, discovered by [Lewis Stiller](/Lewis_Stiller "Lewis Stiller") in 1991 [[4]](#cite_note-4). In March 2006, the wizards of 7-men endgames, Marc Bourzutschky and Yakov Konoval found a 330 moves win in KQBNkqb [[5]](#cite_note-5) and in May 2006 a 517 moves win in KQNkrbn [[6]](#cite_note-6) [[7]](#cite_note-7).
