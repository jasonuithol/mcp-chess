**Milos Tatarevic**, (Miloš Tatarević)  
a Serbian mathematician and computer scientist, residing in the [San Francisco Bay Area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay_Area), [California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California). Currently working as software engineer at *CoinList* [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3), he was previously affiliated with [AngelList](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AngelList) and *Vast* [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5), where he developed their high performance [search engine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_search_engine) in [C](/C "C"). Milos Tatarevic is interested in [combinatorial optimization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combinatorial_optimization), [data mining](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_mining), [problem solving](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Problem_solving), [experimental music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experimental_music) [composing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musical_composition) [[6]](#cite_note-6), and computer chess.

# Xiphos

Milos Tatarevic is author of the [UCI](/UCI "UCI") compliant [open source chess engine](/Category:Open_Source "Category:Open Source") [Xiphos](/Xiphos "Xiphos"). Inspired by [Garry Kasparov's](/Garry_Kasparov "Garry Kasparov") book *Deep Thinking: Where Machine Intelligence Ends and Human Creativity Begins* [[7]](#cite_note-7) [[8]](#cite_note-8), which evoked his childhood passion computer chess, he wondered how hard it could be to develop an engine strong enough to suppress the legendary [Deep Blue](/Deep_Blue "Deep Blue") and gave it a try [[9]](#cite_note-9).
