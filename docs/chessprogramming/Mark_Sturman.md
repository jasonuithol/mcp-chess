**Mark J. Sturman**,  
an American chess player [[2]](#cite_note-2) , chess and computer chess researcher, international chess arbiter and [USCF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Chess_Federation) local tournament director of [North Carolina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Carolina) [[3]](#cite_note-3) . In his 1995 [Computer Chess Reports](/Computer_Chess_Reports "Computer Chess Reports") article *Beware The Bishop Pair*, he elaborated on the relative strength of [bishop versus knight](/Bishop_versus_Knight "Bishop versus Knight") with special mention of the [bishop pair](/Bishop_Pair "Bishop Pair") dependent on other piece configurations and the number of pawns on each side, mining a database of over 350,000 games. A revised version further covering two rooks versus queen was published in the [ICCA Journal, Vol. 19, No. 2](/ICGA_Journal#19_2 "ICGA Journal").
