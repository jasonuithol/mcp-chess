**LambChop**,  
a [Chess Engine Communication Protocol](/Chess_Engine_Communication_Protocol "Chess Engine Communication Protocol") aka [WinBoard](/WinBoard "WinBoard"), and later [UCI](/UCI "UCI") compatible chess engine by [Peter McKenzie](/Peter_McKenzie "Peter McKenzie").
LambChop 10.88 was one of the WinBoard Edition II programs [[2]](#cite_note-2) , commercially available by [Gambit-Soft](/index.php?title=Gambit-Soft&action=edit&redlink=1 "Gambit-Soft (page does not exist)") [[3]](#cite_note-3) , as of May 19, 2002, Lambchop 10.88 was released for free [[4]](#cite_note-4). LambChop played the [WCCC 1999](/WCCC_1999 "WCCC 1999") in [Paderborn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paderborn), and the [CCT1](/CCT1 "CCT1"), [CCT2](/CCT2 "CCT2") [[5]](#cite_note-5) and [CCT3](/CCT3 "CCT3") [[6]](#cite_note-6) [CCT Tournaments](/CCT_Tournaments "CCT Tournaments") until its successor [Warp](/Warp "Warp") became active.

# Description

from the [ICGA](/ICGA "ICGA") tournament site [[8]](#cite_note-8)

```
LambChop is a conventional chess program based around alpha beta searching and iterative deepening, plus the standard tricks such as hash tables, quiescence search, search extensions etc. LambChop can be grouped with those programs that use a combination of recursive nullmove pruning and full endpoint evaluation. Extensive use is made of square control and mobility throughout the program which contributes to an active playing style. The overall design philosophy is focused on the accurate processing of each node as opposed to emphasizing a high NPS rate.
```
