László Lindner [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**László Lindner**, ( 23. Dezember 1916 - 21. August 2004)  
was a Hungarian [Doctor of Laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doctor_of_Laws), chess master and [chess problem composer](/Chess_Problems,_Compositions_and_Studies "Chess Problems, Compositions and Studies") [[2]](#cite_note-2). During [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II) in 1944, when [Nazi Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nazi_Germany) occupied [Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungary) [[3]](#cite_note-3) , Lindner, who was of [Jewish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jews) origin, came under pressure and as a Doctor of Laws he learnt to make butter and cheese in order to get a visa to emigrate to Australia. He got arrested and deported for compulsory labor in the copper mines of [Bor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bor,_Serbia), [Serbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbia), and on September 30, 1944, his group was liberated by [Yugoslav partisans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yugoslav_Partisans) [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5). László Lindner was a proponent of computer chess as a chess problem-solving tool. He organized the [WMCCC 1983](/WMCCC_1983 "WMCCC 1983") in [Budapest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest), and was contributor of three [Advances in Computer Chess Conferences](/Conferences "Conferences"), [ACC 4](/Advances_in_Computer_Chess_4 "Advances in Computer Chess 4"), [ACC 5](/Advances_in_Computer_Chess_5 "Advances in Computer Chess 5") and [ACC 6](/Advances_in_Computer_Chess_6 "Advances in Computer Chess 6"), as well as visitor and guest of later CC conferences and [Tournaments](/Tournaments_and_Matches "Tournaments and Matches").
