**Maurizio Monge**,  
an Italian mathematician and computer scientist, Ph.D. in 2012 from [Scuola Normale Superiore di Pisa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scuola_Normale_Superiore_di_Pisa) under supervision of [Roberto Dvornicich](/Mathematician#RDvornicich "Mathematician") [[2]](#cite_note-2).
His research interests include [extensions of local fields](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finite_extensions_of_local_fields), [Galois modules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galois_module), structure of [recurrence sequences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recurrence_relation), arithmetic of [function field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field_%28mathematics%29#Field_of_functions), and more recently [dynamical systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamical_system) and [noise-induced order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noise-induced_order).

# Computer Chess

Interested in computer chess and programming, Maurizio Monge developed his chess engines [RattateChess](/RattateChess "RattateChess") and [RattatAjedrez](/index.php?title=RattatAjedrez&action=edit&redlink=1 "RattatAjedrez (page does not exist)") [[3]](#cite_note-3).
He has further created various chess [piece sets](/Pieces "Pieces") using [Inkscape](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inkscape), available as [SVG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scalable_Vector_Graphics) source, processed [PNG files](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portable_Network_Graphics) and [XBoard](/XBoard "XBoard") theme [[4]](#cite_note-4).

# Perfect Hashing

Motivated by the application of [magic bitboards](/Magic_Bitboards "Magic Bitboards"), Maurizio worked on its generalization of [perfect hashing](/Hash_Table#PerfectHashing "Hash Table") of numbers with sparse digit representation via multiplication by a constant, elaborating on [Convolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convolution), [Toeplitz matrix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toeplitz_matrix), and [Schur polynomial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schur_polynomial), published in 2010 on [arXiv](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ArXiv) [[5]](#cite_note-5), amd in 2011 in [Discrete Applied Mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrete_Applied_Mathematics), Vol. 159, No. 11.
