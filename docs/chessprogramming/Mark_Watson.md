**Mark Watson**,  
an American computer scientist, programmer, consultant and author of books on [Artificial Intelligence](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence"), [Java](/Java "Java"), [Ruby](/index.php?title=Ruby&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ruby (page does not exist)"), [Common LISP](/index.php?title=Lisp&action=edit&redlink=1 "Lisp (page does not exist)"), [Semantic Web](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semantic_Web), [NLP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuro-linguistic_programming), [C++](/Cpp "Cpp"), [Linux](/Linux "Linux"), [Scheme](/index.php?title=Scheme&action=edit&redlink=1 "Scheme (page does not exist)"), and [Windows](/Windows "Windows").

# Go and Chess

Mark Watson is an avid Chess and [Go](/Go "Go") player, and wrote the first commercial Go playing program, *Honninbo Warrior* for the [Apple II](/Apple_II "Apple II") in the late 1970s [[2]](#cite_note-2). He also wrote the free [Basic](/Basic "Basic") chess program, [Apple](/index.php?title=Apple&action=edit&redlink=1 "Apple (page does not exist)") distributed on their Apple II demo cassette tape [[3]](#cite_note-3). [Mark Watson Chess](/Mark_Watson_Chess "Mark Watson Chess") participated at the [Second West Coast Computer Faire Microcomputer Chess Tournament](/MCCT_1978 "MCCT 1978") in March 1978 in the class C section [[4]](#cite_note-4). His [artificial intelligence](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") [programming](/Programming "Programming") with [Java](/Java "Java") textbook [[5]](#cite_note-5), accompanied with an associated [GitHub](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GitHub) source code repository [[6]](#cite_note-6), comes with a Java chess program skeleton [[7]](#cite_note-7) and much more AI-topics under the [Apache 2.0 License](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apache_License) or under the [GNU Lesser General Public License 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU_Lesser_General_Public_License) [[8]](#cite_note-8).
