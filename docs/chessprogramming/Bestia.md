**Bestia**,  
a [Chess Engine Communication Protocol](/Chess_Engine_Communication_Protocol "Chess Engine Communication Protocol") compliant chess engine by [George Lyapko](/George_Lyapko "George Lyapko"), written in [Pascal](/Pascal "Pascal") and compiled with [Delphi](/Delphi "Delphi") [[2]](#cite_note-2), first released in July 2000 [[3]](#cite_note-3), with executables to run under [Windows](/Windows "Windows"), and more recently, to [x86-64](/X86-64 "X86-64") [Mac OS](/Mac_OS "Mac OS") [[4]](#cite_note-4).
Beside the [transposition table](/Transposition_Table "Transposition Table"), Bestia features a [pawn hash table](/Pawn_Hash_Table "Pawn Hash Table"), and is able to [learn](/Learning "Learning") from its failures [[5]](#cite_note-5).
Bestia participated at three [Hungarian Chessprogram Open](/Hungarian_Chessprogram_Open "Hungarian Chessprogram Open"), and won two times operated by [Gábor Szõts](/Gabor_Szots "Gabor Szots"), the [MASPV 2001](/MASPV_2001 "MASPV 2001") and the [MASPV 2003](/MASPV_2003 "MASPV 2003"), both with a 100% score.
