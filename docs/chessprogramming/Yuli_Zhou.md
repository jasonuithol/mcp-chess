Yuli Zhou [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Yuli Zhou**,  
a [Chinese American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_American) computer scientist and [porfolio manager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portfolio_manager) at [Prediction Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prediction_Company).
He holds a B.S. in electronic engineering and computer science from [University of Science and Technology of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Science_and_Technology_of_China),
and a Ph.D. in [applied mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_mathematics) and computer science from [Harvard University](/Harvard_University "Harvard University") [[2]](#cite_note-2).
From 1990 to '95 he worked at [MIT](/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") on [massively parallel computing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massively_parallel_%28computing%29) and contributed to [Cilk](/Cilk "Cilk") and its first chess program [\*Socrates](/Star_Socrates "Star Socrates").

# Quotes

[Chris Joerg's](/Chris_Joerg "Chris Joerg") and [Bradley Kuszmaul's](/Bradley_Kuszmaul "Bradley Kuszmaul") acknowledgments in *Massively Parallel Chess* on [\*Socrates](/Star_Socrates "Star Socrates") [[3]](#cite_note-3): [Charles E. Leiserson](/Charles_Leiserson "Charles Leiserson"), [Robert D. Blumofe](/Robert_Blumofe "Robert Blumofe"), Yuli Zhou, and [Michael Halbherr](/Michael_Halbherr "Michael Halbherr") all contributed to making the chess program work and to developing the underlying parallel technology used in \*Socrates. [Don Dailey](/Don_Dailey "Don Dailey") and [Larry Kaufman](/Larry_Kaufman "Larry Kaufman") of [Heuristic Software](/Heuristic_Software "Heuristic Software") provided the serial program, [Socrates](/Socrates "Socrates"), on which our parallel program is based, and Don worked many hours to help us get our parallel program working. [Hans Berliner](/Hans_Berliner "Hans Berliner") and [Chris McConnell](/Chris_McConnell "Chris McConnell") of [CMU](/Carnegie_Mellon_University "Carnegie Mellon University") provided the serial version of [Hitech](/HiTech "HiTech") that we first used as a testbed to develop our ideas for [parallel game tree search](/Parallel_Search "Parallel Search").
