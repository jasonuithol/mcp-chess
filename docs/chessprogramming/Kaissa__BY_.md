# Kaissa (BY)

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Kaissa_(BY)

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Engines](/Engines "Engines") \* Kaissa (BY)**

**Kaissa**, (Kaissa2)  
a [WinBoard](/WinBoard "WinBoard") compliant chess engine by [Vladimir Yelin](/Vladimir_Yelin "Vladimir Yelin"), [namesake](/Category:Namesake "Category:Namesake") of the first [World Computer Chess Champion](/WCCC_1974 "WCCC 1974"),
the famous [Kaissa](/Kaissa "Kaissa"), and at times considered [Crafty](/Crafty "Crafty") [clone](/Category:Clone "Category:Clone") [[1]](#cite_note-1).
[Sergei S. Markoff](/Sergei_Markoff "Sergei Markoff") mentioned Vladimir Yelin also using ideas by [Mikhail Botvinnik](/Mikhail_Botvinnik "Mikhail Botvinnik") to determine objects for attack end defence
[[2]](#cite_note-2)
[[3]](#cite_note-3).
In 2004, Kaissa2 evolved to [Patriot](/Patriot "Patriot") to become a commercial [Arena](/Arena "Arena") engine. In May 2005, the new Patriot 2.0 turned out to be a [Fruit](/Fruit "Fruit")/[Toga](/Toga "Toga") [clone](/Category:Clone "Category:Clone")
[[4]](#cite_note-4)
[[5]](#cite_note-5)
[[6]](#cite_note-6),
when later investigations revealed the previously assumed innocent
[[7]](#cite_note-7)
Patriot 1 aka later Kaissa versions were based on [Crafty](/Crafty "Crafty") with many changes.
Investigator [Alexander Schmidt](/index.php?title=Alexander_Schmidt&action=edit&redlink=1 "Alexander Schmidt (page does not exist)") stated that later versions of Kaissa released after the Patriot disaster still have Crafty traces inside
[[8]](#cite_note-8).
