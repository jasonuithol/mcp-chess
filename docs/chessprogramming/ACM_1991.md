[ACM's](/ACM "ACM") **22nd North American Computer Chess Championship** (NACCC), alongside the [ACM/IEEE Supercomputing Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ACM/IEEE_Supercomputing_Conference), was held November 17-20, [1991](/Timeline#1991 "Timeline") at the Doubletree Hotel, [Albuquerque](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albuquerque%2C_New_Mexico), [New Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Mexico), USA.

# Where is Computer Chess Going?

A technical session titled *Where is Computer Chess Going* was moderated by [Tony Marsland](/Tony_Marsland "Tony Marsland"), with [Hans Berliner](/Hans_Berliner "Hans Berliner"), [Monroe Newborn](/Monroe_Newborn "Monroe Newborn"), [Murray Campbell](/Murray_Campbell "Murray Campbell"), [Robert Levinson](/Robert_Levinson "Robert Levinson") and [Jaap van den Herik](/Jaap_van_den_Herik "Jaap van den Herik") attending as panelist [[6]](#cite_note-6) . At one point the discussion went on [brute strength](/Brute-Force "Brute-Force") versus [trimming](/Pruning "Pruning") techniques - Campbell replied that both approaches will eventually succeed. [Larry Kaufman](/Larry_Kaufman "Larry Kaufman") commented from the audience that [selective](/Selectivity "Selectivity") [searches](/Search "Search") outperform full width on comparable hardware. [Don Dailey](/Don_Dailey "Don Dailey") mentioned that one main goal is to beat the World Champion. He asked "How long will it take to cross the extra distance and play a perfect game?". Berliner replied, "Centuries! A perfect game is a science-fiction dream". Further, Robert Levinson gave a presentation of his [Morph](/Morph "Morph") project.
