**Carl Ebeling**,  
an American computer scientist, electrical engineer, and professor at the Department of Computer Science & Engineering, [University of Washington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Washington), [Seattle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle%2C_Washington).

# All the Right Moves

As Ph.D. student at [Carnegie Mellon University](/Carnegie_Mellon_University "Carnegie Mellon University"), Carl Ebeling was member of the [HiTech](/HiTech "HiTech") team around [Hans Berliner](/Hans_Berliner "Hans Berliner"). Carl Ebeling's Ph.D. thesis was titled *All the Right Moves* [[2]](#cite_note-2) and describes a [VLSI architecture](/VLSI_Design "VLSI Design") for computing complex chess [evaluation functions](/Evaluation_Function "Evaluation Function") quickly, as used in HiTech.

# Quotes

[Hans Berliner](/Hans_Berliner "Hans Berliner") on Ebeling's role in the HiTech team (1988) [[4]](#cite_note-4) :

```
Carl Ebeling (now at University of Washington) built the special purpose hardware (Ebeling 1985), and a good deal of software relating to how to interface to the hardware and see what it is doing for debugging purposes.
```
