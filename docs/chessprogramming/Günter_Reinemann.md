Günter Reinemann [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Günter Reinemann**, (January 26, 1942 - July 16, 2018 [[2]](#cite_note-2))  
was a German chess official, computer scientist, during the 60s affiliated with the department of computing technology at [TU Merseburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technical_University_Leuna-Merseburg) and head of their [data center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_center) [[3]](#cite_note-3), as well as computer chess expert and co-author of computer chess books. Günter Reinemann was honorary president and senior chess consultant of the Chess Federation of [Saxony-Anhalt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saxony-Anhalt) [[4]](#cite_note-4), and co-founder of the *[Emanuel Lasker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emanuel_Lasker) Society* in 2001 [[5]](#cite_note-5) [[6]](#cite_note-6).
