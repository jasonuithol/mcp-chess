Manlio Morini [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Manlio Morini**,  
an Italian software developer, operating *EOS Development* developing and providing software for businesses and developers, IT consultancy, maintenance and repair of hardware components and their supply in specific projects.
In the late 90s, he developed the [Chess Engine Communication Protocol](/Chess_Engine_Communication_Protocol "Chess Engine Communication Protocol") compliant chess engine [Joker](/Joker_IT "Joker IT"), in 2017 published on [Github](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GitHub) as [open source](/Category:Open_Source "Category:Open Source") as "historical" artifact, under the terms of the [MIT license](/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology#License "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") [[2]](#cite_note-2).
His new open source engine [Testudo](/index.php?title=Testudo&action=edit&redlink=1 "Testudo (page does not exist)") was designed with the goals of [simplicity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplicity) and [correctness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correctness_(computer_science)) in mind, and interfaces with his [genetic programming](/Genetic_Programming "Genetic Programming") framework **Vita** [[3]](#cite_note-3) to establish a flexible test-bed for [artificial intelligence](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") and [machine learning](/Learning "Learning") techniques [[4]](#cite_note-4).
