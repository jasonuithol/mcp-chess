**MCCT 1978**,  
the World's **First Microcomputer Chess Tournament** took place from March 3-5, [1978](/Timeline#1978 "Timeline"), during the Second [West Coast Computer Faire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Coast_Computer_Faire), [San Jose Convention Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Jose_Convention_Center), [San Jose, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Jose,_California). At the [ACM 1977](/ACM_1977 "ACM 1977"), [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy") made the suggestion to organize a Microcomputer Chess Tournament to [Douglas Penrod](/Douglas_Penrod "Douglas Penrod"), editor of the [Computer Chess Newsletter](/ICGA_Journal#ComputerChessNewsletter "ICGA Journal") [[2]](#cite_note-2) . Therefor, the Second West Coast Computer Faire hosted the event, announced January 1978 in the *Silicon Gulch Gazette*, a free publication associated with the Faire, edited by [Jim Warren](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jim_Warren_%28computer_specialist%29) [[3]](#cite_note-3) [[4]](#cite_note-4) . [Larry Wagner](/Larry_Wagner "Larry Wagner"), at that time affiliated with [Atari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atari_Corporation), served as tournament director. The winning program, called [Sargon](/Sargon "Sargon"), was a 16 Kbyte [Z-80](/Z80 "Z80") [assembler](/Assembly "Assembly") program written by a husband and wife team, [Kathe](/Kathe_Spracklen "Kathe Spracklen") and [Dan Spracklen](/Dan_Spracklen "Dan Spracklen") [[5]](#cite_note-5) [[6]](#cite_note-6) .
