Roma 68020 [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Mephisto Roma**,  
a set of [dedicated chess computer](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") [modules](/Module "Module") by [Hegener & Glaser](/Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser") released in 1987 for their [Mephisto module systems](/Mephisto_Module_Systems "Mephisto Module Systems"), like its predecessor [Mephisto Dallas](/Mephisto_Dallas "Mephisto Dallas") as [68000](/68000 "68000") and [68020](/68020 "68020") versions @ 12 and 14 MHz with 16 and 64 KiB of [RAM](/Memory#RAM "Memory") respectively. Thus, the program was the further development of [Richard Lang's](/Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") Mephisto series, winning the third consecutive title at the [WMCCC 1987](/WMCCC_1987 "WMCCC 1987") in [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome) ([Roma](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roma) in Italian), again the site eponym of the computer modules. The [Manufacturers Championship](/WMCCC_1987#Commercial "WMCCC 1987") was a duel between [Hegener & Glaser](/Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser") and [Newcrest Technology](/Newcrest_Technology "Newcrest Technology"), where three Mephisto Roma on 68020 @ 25 or 28 MHz [[2]](#cite_note-2) outclassed three [CXG Sphinx](/CXG_Sphinx "CXG Sphinx") with a [Intelligent Software](/Intelligent_Software "Intelligent Software") program by [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy") and [Mark Taylor](/Mark_Taylor "Mark Taylor"), running on a 68020 as well, with a stunning 9:0 result - while the games were more closely than the results suggested [[3]](#cite_note-3). The cheaper Roma II [68000](/68000 "68000") module was released two years later.

# Features

[Kaare Danielsen](/Kaare_Danielsen "Kaare Danielsen") praised [Lang's](/Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") programs in general in his [ICCA Journal](/ICGA_Journal#10_3 "ICGA Journal") report of the [Rome WMCCC](/WMCCC_1987 "WMCCC 1987"), where [Psion](/Psion "Psion"), still Lang's development base for his Mephisto programs, also won the Software group [[4]](#cite_note-4):

```
Clearly, Richard Lang has produced the strongest microcomputer program in the world. It searches 4-5 plies brute force plus up to 6-7 plies selective search. The program contains a lot of chess knowledge, which is used both in the tree and to create piece-value-tables. The chess knowledge was obtained from chess masters, who analyzed games played by the program. The program has an asymmetric evaluation function, which emphasis safety (has rules like 'do not start a wild attack, but worry a lot if the opponent does so'). The program searches about 4000 nodes per second on a 28 MHz 68020, but this includes a thorough analysis at every node, including a swap-off evaluation (static analysis of captures instead of a capture search).
```

# Improvements

Monsieur Plastique recalled the original [Mephisto Dallas](/Mephisto_Dallas "Mephisto Dallas") to Roma evolution was considered to be an extremely small one [[5]](#cite_note-5). Improvements of [middlegame](/Middlegame "Middlegame") and [endgame](/Endgame "Endgame") and Blitzchess mentioned in the introduction of the Roma manual [[6]](#cite_note-6) look like a plain translation from the German Dallas manual [[7]](#cite_note-7).
