**Claude Jarry**,  
a Canadian computer scientist at [Commission scolaire de Montréal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commission_scolaire_de_Montr%C3%A9al), B.Sc. from [McGill University](/McGill_University "McGill University") in 1978.
While affiliated with McGill as student of [Monroe Newborn](/Monroe_Newborn "Monroe Newborn"), he began chess programming in 1976 as a class project [[2]](#cite_note-2).
His program dubbed [L'Excentrique](/L%27Excentrique "L'Excentrique") ran on [Amdahl](/Amdahl_470 "Amdahl 470") mainframe computers [[3]](#cite_note-3), at the [WCCC 1980](/WCCC_1980 "WCCC 1980") in [Linz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linz) with the first round victory versus [Chess 4.9](/Chess_(Program) "Chess (Program)"),
becoming strong fourth [[4]](#cite_note-4).
