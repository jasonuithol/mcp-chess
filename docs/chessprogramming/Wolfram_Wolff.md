**Wolfram Wolff**,  
a German computer scientist and former chess programmer. While affiliated with the [Technical University of Munich](/Technical_University_of_Munich "Technical University of Munich") during the late 70s, he worked with [Thomas Nitsche](/Thomas_Nitsche "Thomas Nitsche") and [Elmar Henne](/Elmar_Henne "Elmar Henne") on the parallel chess program [Parwell](/Parwell "Parwell"), which performed a [distributed search](/Parallel_Search "Parallel Search") on a 128 processor [Siemens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siemens) [SMS 201](/SMS_201 "SMS 201") computer. Parwell played the [ECCC 1979](/ECCC_1979 "ECCC 1979") in [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London) [[1]](#cite_note-1), and the [WCCC 1980](/WCCC_1980 "WCCC 1980") in [Linz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linz) [[2]](#cite_note-2).
