Barbican Centre [[1]](#cite_note-1)

The inofficial **3rd European Microcomputer Chess Championship** was held September 9-12, [1982](/Timeline#1982 "Timeline") at the [Barbican Centre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbican_Centre) in [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London), [United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom) as part of the [Personal Computer World Show](/Personal_Computer_World "Personal Computer World") [[2]](#cite_note-2), also known as the 5th [PCW Microcomputer Chess Championship](/PCW_Microcomputer_Chess_Championship "PCW Microcomputer Chess Championship").

Due to time pressure, it was decided to [adjudicate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adjournment_%28games%29) all unfinished games at move 53. This set the tournament director, [Stuart Reuben](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stewart_Reuben), some complicated adjudication problems in the course of the four days. Sole winner with 6/7 was [Advance 2.4](/Advance "Advance") by [Dave Wilson](/Dave_Wilson "Dave Wilson") and [Mike Johnson](/Mike_Johnson "Mike Johnson"), running on a home constructed [bit-slice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bit_slicing) machine [[3]](#cite_note-3).
