Victor Zakharov [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Victor Zakharov**,  
a Russian computer scientist holding a Ph.D. in CS from [Lomonosov Moscow State University](/Moscow_State_University "Moscow State University"), entrepreneur, founder of [ChessOK](/ChessOK "ChessOK") aka **Convekta**, head of its development, primary developer of [Chess Assistant](/Chess_Assistant "Chess Assistant") [[2]](#cite_note-2) and [Aquarium](/Aquarium "Aquarium") [[3]](#cite_note-3), and scientific secretary at the *Institute of Informatics Problems* of the [Russian Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Academy_of_Sciences), [Moscow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moscow) [[4]](#cite_note-4). Also affiliated with the Lomonosov Moscow State University, Victor Zakharov is along with [Vladimir Makhnychev](/Vladimir_Makhnychev "Vladimir Makhnychev") involved in generating the [7-men](/Endgame_Tablebases#7-men "Endgame Tablebases") [Lomonosov Tablebases](/Lomonosov_Tablebases "Lomonosov Tablebases") [[5]](#cite_note-5), using the [Lomonosov Supercomputer](/Lomonosov_Supercomputer "Lomonosov Supercomputer"). As of December 31, 2013, the tablebases are commercially available online through [ChessOK](/ChessOK "ChessOK") [products](/ChessOK#Products "ChessOK") [[6]](#cite_note-6).
