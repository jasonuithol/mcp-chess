**Space**,  
a loosely defined evaluation feature related to [square control](/Square_Control "Square Control"), in particular [center control](/Center_Control "Center Control") considered by [piece placement](/Piece-Square_Tables "Piece-Square Tables") dependent on [pawn structure](/Pawn_Structure "Pawn Structure"). A player controlling more squares than the other is said to have a spatial advantage [[2]](#cite_note-2). [Tarrasch's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siegbert_Tarrasch) concept of force, space and time [[3]](#cite_note-3) and their equivalence (to some extend) is considered by [material](/Material "Material") (force), piece placement and center control (space) and roughly by [mobility](/Mobility "Mobility") (space and time).

# Implementations

Some programs have explicit evaluation terms concerning space. For instance, [Stockfish](/Stockfish "Stockfish") defines a space area bonus by the number of safe squares for minor pieces on the central four files on ranks 2 to 4, counting twice if on a [rearspan](/Pawn_Spans "Pawn Spans") of an own pawn. The space area bonus is multiplied by a weight, determined by the number of own pieces minus number of open files [[4]](#cite_note-4). [Toga II](/Toga "Toga") 3.0 gives a non-linear bonus for pieces on the opponent half of the board - the more the better [[5]](#cite_note-5). [Senpai 2.0](/Senpai "Senpai") considers space by the "glorified" [pawn chain](/Pawn_Chain "Pawn Chain") [piece-square tables](/Piece-Square_Tables "Piece-Square Tables") [[6]](#cite_note-6).
