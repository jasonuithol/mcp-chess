Saitek PC Auto Chessboard [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Saitek PC Auto Chessboard**, (Kasparov PC Auto Chessboard, Mephisto PC Schachbrett)  
a magnetic sensory wooden chess board released by [Saitek](/Saitek "Saitek") in 1995 as [dedicated chess](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") user interface for the [IBM PC](/IBM_PC "IBM PC"),
connected via the [parallel port](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallel_port).
The board was no own development but the housing of the [Saitek President](/Saitek_President "Saitek President") [[2]](#cite_note-2),
and was bundled with [Ed Schröder's](/Ed_Schroder "Ed Schroder") [MS-DOS](/MS-DOS "MS-DOS") program [Mephisto Advantage](/Gideon#MephistoGideon "Gideon") [[3]](#cite_note-3) and was further compatible with [Genius 3](/Chess_Genius "Chess Genius"), [Fritz 3](/Fritz "Fritz"), [Rebel 6](/Rebel "Rebel") and subsequent versions, and [Virtua Chess](/Virtua_Chess "Virtua Chess").
The board was shipped under the well known [Mephisto](/Mephisto "Mephisto") trademark in German speaking countries as **Mephisto PC Schachbrett**, after Saitek acquired [Hegener & Glaser](/Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser") in 1994. [Nicolas Cominetti](/index.php?title=Nicolas_Cominetti&action=edit&redlink=1 "Nicolas Cominetti (page does not exist)") has written a [WinBoard](/WinBoard "WinBoard") driver dubbed **Autowb**, hosted by [Tim Mann](/Tim_Mann "Tim Mann") [[4]](#cite_note-4), that made the Saitek Board [WinBoard](/WinBoard "WinBoard") compatible.
