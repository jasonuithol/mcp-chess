**Bradley C. Kuszmaul**,  
an American computer scientist and programmer at in the Supercomputing Technologies Group at the [MIT](/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") Laboratory for Computer Science. He was involved in [Akamai Technologies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akamai_Technologies) and a couple of startups: *Tokutek* and [Cilk Arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cilk). Bradley Kuszmaul was one of the principal architects of the [Connection Machine CM-5](/Connection_Machine "Connection Machine"), participated at MIT at the [Cilk](/Cilk "Cilk") project [[2]](#cite_note-2), introduced the [Jamboree algorithm](/Jamboree "Jamboree") in his Ph.D. thesis [[3]](#cite_note-3) and is co-author of two world-class computer chess programs applying this algorithm, [StarTech](/StarTech "StarTech") and [\*Socrates](/Star_Socrates "Star Socrates") [[4]](#cite_note-4).
