**Excalibur King Arthur**,  
a [dedicated chess computer](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") by [Excalibur Electronics](/Excalibur_Electronics "Excalibur Electronics"), released in 2000 [[2]](#cite_note-2).
The computer has a [Samsung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Electronics) S3C72P9 controller with a [SAM47](/SAM "SAM") 4-bit core at 1.5 MHz with 32 KiB [ROM](/Memory#ROM "Memory") and 1056 [nibbles](/Nibble "Nibble") of [RAM](/Memory#RAM "Memory"),
and features a press [sensory board](/Sensory_Board "Sensory Board") along with a [LCD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid-crystal_display) board and button panel.
The 4-bit program, developed by [Ron Nelson](/Ron_Nelson "Ron Nelson") during his [sabbatical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabbatical) in late 1999 for the [Excalibur LCD Chess](/Excalibur_LCD_Chess "Excalibur LCD Chess") [[3]](#cite_note-3),
applies sophisticated [PV move](/PV-Move "PV-Move") and [killer move](/Killer_Move "Killer Move") [ordering heuristics](/Move_Ordering "Move Ordering") [[4]](#cite_note-4),
and was slightly customized for the new hardware.
