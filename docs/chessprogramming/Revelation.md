**Revelation**,  
a dedicated chess computer system by [Phoenix Chess Systems](/index.php?title=Phoenix_Chess_Systems&action=edit&redlink=1 "Phoenix Chess Systems (page does not exist)"), developed by its founder and CEO [Ruud Martin](/Ruud_Martin "Ruud Martin"). While a prototype was introduced in 2006 [[3]](#cite_note-3), the Revelation was first shipped in June 2008 [[4]](#cite_note-4) and superseded by its successor [Revelation II](/Revelation_II "Revelation II") in 2013. The system features a [sensory board](/Sensory_Board "Sensory Board"), an [XScale](/index.php?title=XScale&action=edit&redlink=1 "XScale (page does not exist)") [PXA255](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/XScale#PXA210.2FPXA25x) 32-bit processor at 500 MHz with 32 MiB of [RAM](/Memory#RAM "Memory"), and [firmware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firmware) and chess engines in [non-volatile flash memory](/Memory#ROM "Memory"). In 2009, the Revelation also appeared as [module](/Module "Module") set for [Mephisto boards](/Mephisto#boards "Mephisto") [[5]](#cite_note-5).

# Panel

[![](/images/thumb/e/e3/RevelationGUI.jpg/300px-RevelationGUI.jpg)](https://www.flickr.com/photos/10261668@N05/2179418372/in/album-72157602195096455/)

Revelation's [OLED](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OLED) display screen [[6]](#cite_note-6)

A six button and [OLED](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OLED) panel realize a [menu-driven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Menu_(computing)) [graphical user interface](/GUI "GUI") to configure and choose engines, and to control and indicate system modes and states. A wireless [Bluetooth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluetooth) connection allows native engine or firmware updates, and due to the [UCI](/UCI "UCI") server, also allows using the Revelation as dedicated [user interface](/User_Interface "User Interface") for [UCI engines](/Category:UCI "Category:UCI") running on a [PC](/IBM_PC "IBM PC").
