Árpád Rusz [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Árpád Rusz**,  
a Romanian chess player [[2]](#cite_note-2) and [chess endgame study composer](/Category:Chess_Composer "Category:Chess Composer") [[3]](#cite_note-3) . He contributed along with [Guy Haworth](/Guy_Haworth "Guy Haworth") on *Position Criticality* in Chess Endgames at the [Advances in Computer Games 13](/Advances_in_Computer_Games_13 "Advances in Computer Games 13") conference in [Tilburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tilburg) [[4]](#cite_note-4) , demonstrating the implementation of their algorithm to help analyze uniqueness in endgame positions objectively with a chess variant called [Starchess](/index.php?title=Starchess&action=edit&redlink=1 "Starchess (page does not exist)") [[5]](#cite_note-5) , invented by [László Polgár](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A1szl%C3%B3_Polg%C3%A1r) in 2002, which has a star-shaped board of 37 [hexagons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hexagon) [[6]](#cite_note-6).
