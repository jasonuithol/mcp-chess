Vivien Clauzon [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Vivien Clauzon**,  
a French software architect and applied mathematician holding a Ph.D. from [Clermont-Ferrand II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blaise_Pascal_University) on the topic of [computational fluid dynamics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_fluid_dynamics) in 2008.
He further elaborated on [L∞ stability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L-stability) of the [MUSCL method](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MUSCL_scheme) in the study of [partial differential equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partial_differential_equation).
As a computer chess programmer, Vivien Clauzon is author of the chess engines [Weini](/Weini "Weini") and [Minic](/Minic "Minic").
