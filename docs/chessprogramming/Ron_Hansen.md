**Ron Hansen**,  
an American computer scientist and early computer chess programmer when he was undergraduate student at [University of Waterloo](/University_of_Waterloo "University of Waterloo") in the mid 70s. Along with [Jim Parry](/Jim_Parry "Jim Parry"), [Russell Crook](/Russell_Crook "Russell Crook"), and [Gary Calnek](/Gary_Calnek "Gary Calnek"), he was co-author of the successful mainframe programs [Ribbit](/Ribbit "Ribbit") [[1]](#cite_note-1) and its successor [Treefrog](/Treefrog "Treefrog") [[2]](#cite_note-2), and wrote a master thesis on computer chess [[3]](#cite_note-3). At his time at Waterloo, [Jonathan Schaeffer](/Jonathan_Schaeffer "Jonathan Schaeffer") benefited from the presence of Ron Hansen, who generously gave him a copy of Ribbit's [Fortran](/Fortran "Fortran") source code, which he used to learn how to write a chess program [[4]](#cite_note-4).
