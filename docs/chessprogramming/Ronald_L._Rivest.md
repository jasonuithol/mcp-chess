Ronald L. Rivest [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Ronald (Ron) Linn Rivest**,  
an American mathematician, computer scientist and [cryptographer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryptography),
[Andrew and Erna Viterbi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_Viterbi) Professor of Computer Science and Engineering in [MIT's](/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) and a member of [MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIT_Computer_Science_and_Artificial_Intelligence_Laboratory) (CSAIL).
Ron Rivest is one of the inventors of the [RSA algorithm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RSA) (with [Adi Shamir](/Mathematician#AShamir "Mathematician") and [Leonard Adleman](/Mathematician#LAdleman "Mathematician")) [[2]](#cite_note-2), and inventor of the [symmetric key](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symmetric_key) encryption algorithms [RC2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RC2), [RC4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RC4_%28cipher%29), [RC5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RC5), co-inventor of [RC6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RC6), designer of the [cryptographic hash functions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cryptographic_hash_function) [MD2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MD2_%28cryptography%29), [MD4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MD4) and [MD5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MD5) message-digest algorithms, co-designer of [MD6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MD6), and co-author (with [Charles Leiserson](/Charles_Leiserson "Charles Leiserson") et al.) of [Introduction to Algorithms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Introduction_to_Algorithms).
He further researched and published on [machine learning](/Learning "Learning") and [game tree search](/Search "Search") by Min/Max approximation [[3]](#cite_note-3).
