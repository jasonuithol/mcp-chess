Arthur Norman [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Arthur C. Norman**,  
a British mathematician, computer scientist, and Fellow of [Trinity College, Cambridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trinity_College,_Cambridge),
where he has previously been a director of studies for computer science [[2]](#cite_note-2).
His research interests include [functional programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_programming), [symbolic computation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbolic_computation), [computer algebra systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_algebra_system) and [programming languages](/Languages "Languages").
In the 80s, while also affiliated with [Acornsoft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acornsoft), he was involved in developing the [Acornsoft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acornsoft_LISP) [LISP](/index.php?title=LISP&action=edit&redlink=1 "LISP (page does not exist)") interpreter [[3]](#cite_note-3),
and, along with [Nick Pelling](/Nick_Pelling "Nick Pelling") (Orlando), he contributed to the chess program [Acornsoft Chess](/Acornsoft_Chess "Acornsoft Chess") [[4]](#cite_note-4),
both for the [BBC Micro](/BBC_Micro "BBC Micro"). In 1983, along with [John P. Fitch](/Mathematician#JohnFitch "Mathematician"), the Fitch-Norman Partnership was established, which became **Codemist Ltd** in 1988 [[5]](#cite_note-5),
developing and trading the [Norcroft compiler suite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norcroft_C_compiler) targeting [RISC OS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RISC_OS).
