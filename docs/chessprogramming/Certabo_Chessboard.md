Certabo Identifying Chips

The Italian company **Certabo** manufactures and markets electronic **Chessboards** with automatic [piece recognition](/Piece_Recognition "Piece Recognition") to allow playing against strong chess engines on stylish traditional wooden boards. Unlike other electronic boards, Certabo boards do not necessarily come with their own, fixed pieces. Instead, a set of 34 identifying chips is provided, which can be attached to the bottom of pieces in any set. Although mostly intended for chess (32 pieces and 2 additional queens for promotion) [[1]](#cite_note-1) the board can also be used for [8x8 checkers](/Checkers "Checkers") (24 regular checkers and 10 kings for promotion)
[[2]](#cite_note-2).

# Third Party GUIs

Although the company provides its own software
[[3]](#cite_note-3), the open protocol encourages third party [GUIs](/GUI "GUI") to support the board as well. [Aart Bik](/Aart_Bik "Aart Bik")'s [Chess for Android](/Chess_for_Android "Chess for Android") and [Checkers for Android](/index.php?title=Checkers_for_Android&action=edit&redlink=1 "Checkers for Android (page does not exist)") were the first Android GUIs [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5) to support Certabo boards for chess and 8x8 checkers, respectively.

# Maia Chess

**Certabo DaVinci** [[6]](#cite_note-6) and **Certabo Nano** [[7]](#cite_note-7) are [dedicated chess computer](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") [modules](/Module "Module") for the Certabo Chessboard, incorporating [Maia Chess](/Maia_Chess "Maia Chess") as a chess playing engine.
