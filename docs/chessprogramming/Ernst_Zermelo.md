**Ernst Friedrich Ferdinand Zermelo**, (July 27, 1871 – May 21, 1953)

[![](/images/1/12/Zermelo.jpeg)](http://www-history.mcs.st-andrews.ac.uk/PictDisplay/Zermelo.html)

Ernst Zermelo [[1]](#cite_note-1)

a German mathematician and pioneer in [set-](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_theory) and [game theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_theory). His university bibliography includes the [Humboldt University of Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humboldt_University_of_Berlin) 1894, the [University of Göttingen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_G%C3%B6ttingen) 1899, honorary professorship in [Zürich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich) 1910, and the [University of Freiburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Freiburg) since 1926. During the [Third Reich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nazi_Germany), in 1935 disciplinary actions were initiated against him, because he refused the [Hitler salute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hitler_salute). To preempt of his dismissal, as a result he withdrew voluntarily. In 1946 he was reinstated in Freiburg [[2]](#cite_note-2).

# Set Theory

In 1900, Zermelo proved the [well-ordering theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Well-ordering_theorem), which states that every set can be well ordered. This gave rise to the Zermelo axiom that every class can be well ordered. In 1904 Zermelo defined the [axiom of choice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axiom_of_choice) [[3]](#cite_note-3), the use of which had previously been unrecognized in mathematical reasoning. The first formulations of axioms for set theory - an axiom system for German mathematician [Georg Cantor's](/Mathematician#Cantor "Mathematician") theory of sets [[4]](#cite_note-4) - were made by Zermelo in 1908.

# Game Theory

In [1912](/Timeline#1912 "Timeline"), Zermelo proved the determinism of games like chess and that rational players were able to utilize all information to develop an optimal strategy [[5]](#cite_note-5). [Zermelo's theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zermelo%27s_theorem_%28game_theory%29) is the mathematical justification for the [retrograde analysis](/Retrograde_Analysis "Retrograde Analysis") chess algorithm [[6]](#cite_note-6) [[7]](#cite_note-7).

# Tournament Results

Zermelo published a paper about "The calculation of the tournament results as a maximum problem of the probability calculus" in 1929 [[8]](#cite_note-8) .
