**Ed Schröder**, (Ed Schroder)  
a Dutch computer chess programmer and founder of **Schröder BV** [[2]](#cite_note-2), a chess software developing company. Ed is author of various chess programs for PCs as well for [dedicated chess computers](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers"). His most famous chess programs were the commercial programs [Rebel](/Rebel "Rebel") [[3]](#cite_note-3), and based on Rebel the [WMCCC 1991](/WMCCC_1991 "WMCCC 1991") Micro Champion [Gideon](/Gideon "Gideon") [[4]](#cite_note-4), the World Computer Chess Champion from the [WCCC 1992](/WCCC_1992 "WCCC 1992"), [ChessMachine](/ChessMachine "ChessMachine") [[5]](#cite_note-5), and the free program [ProDeo](/ProDeo "ProDeo") [[6]](#cite_note-6) [[7]](#cite_note-7). Ed contributed a lot to computer chess, since he made almost all his knowledge about chess programming [public](/Recommended_Reading "Recommended Reading"), when he retired from competition in 2003 [[8]](#cite_note-8). In 2002 [Lokasoft](/Lokasoft "Lokasoft") took over the activities of **Schröder BV** [[9]](#cite_note-9).

# 2011 Rybka Controversy

In 2011, Ed Schröder first signed the [open letter](/Open_letter_to_the_ICGA_about_the_Rybka-Fruit_issue "Open letter to the ICGA about the Rybka-Fruit issue") directed to the [ICGA](/ICGA "ICGA"), to support the claim [Rybka 1.0 beta](/Rybka "Rybka") and subsequent versions were allegedly derivatives from [Fabien Letouzey’s](/Fabien_Letouzey "Fabien Letouzey") program [Fruit 2.1](/Fruit "Fruit"), but soon after the [ICGA decision](/Rybka_Controversy#RybkaDecision "Rybka Controversy"), reconsidered [[10]](#cite_note-10) [[11]](#cite_note-11), apologized to [Vasik Rajlich](/Vasik_Rajlich "Vasik Rajlich") [[12]](#cite_note-12) and criticized the [investigation process](/ICGA_Investigations "ICGA Investigations") and the represented evidence. In November 2011, Ed Schröder announced his new web-sites [[13]](#cite_note-13) [[14]](#cite_note-14) that give an alternative view of the historic ICGA - RYBKA event that led to the ban of Rybka.
