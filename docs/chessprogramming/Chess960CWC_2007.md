The **Third Livingston** [[1]](#cite_note-1) [Chess960](/Chess960 "Chess960") **Computer World Championship** was held within the scope of the [Chess Classic](http://www.chesstigers.de/ccm9.php?lang=1) [Mainz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainz), Germany, August 15 - 17, [2007](/Timeline#2007 "Timeline"), organized by [Hans-Walter Schmitt](/index.php?title=Hans-Walter_Schmitt&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hans-Walter Schmitt (page does not exist)") and his Chess Tigers [[2]](#cite_note-2). Due to financial constraints the tournament was no longer an open tournament and four programs were invited, [2005](/Chess960CWC_2005 "Chess960CWC 2005") and [2006](/Chess960CWC_2006 "Chess960CWC 2006") champions [Spike](/Spike "Spike") and [Shredder](/Shredder "Shredder"), two times vice champion [Jonny](/Jonny "Jonny"), and current Number One in the [SSDF](/SSDF "SSDF") rating list [[3]](#cite_note-3) and [WCCC 2007](/WCCC_2007 "WCCC 2007") champion [Rybka](/Rybka "Rybka"). [Hans Secelle](/Hans_Secelle "Hans Secelle") served as arbiter, and [Eric van Reem](/Eric_van_Reem "Eric van Reem") as Tournament director.
