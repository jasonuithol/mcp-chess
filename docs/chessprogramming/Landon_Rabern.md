**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* Landon Rabern**

**Landon W. Rabern**,  
an American mathematician and software engineer in the AI industry. His research interests include [combinatorics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combinatorics) and [graph theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graph_theory), such as [clique problems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clique_problem), [graph coloring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graph_coloring) and [edge coloring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edge_coloring).
He holds a B.Sc. from [Washington University in St. Louis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_University_in_St._Louis) in 2003,
a M.Sc. from [University of California, Santa Barbara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Barbara) in 2005, and
a Ph.D. from [Arizona State University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arizona_State_University) in 2013.
As a computer chess programmer, Landon Rabern is author the [WinBoard](/WinBoard "WinBoard") compatible chess engine [Betsy](/Betsy "Betsy") [[1]](#cite_note-1), which was the first published chess engine able to play [Chess960](/Chess960 "Chess960") [[2]](#cite_note-2).
