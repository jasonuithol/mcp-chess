Thomas Jahn [[1]](#cite_note-LinkedIn-1)

**Thomas Jahn**,  
a German computer scientist, game developer and technical director at [King Art Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_Art_Games) in [Bremen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bremen).
He holds a [diplom degree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplom) in media [informatics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Informatics) from [City University of Applied Sciences (Bremen)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/City_University_of_Applied_Sciences_(Bremen)) in 2008 [[1]](#cite_note-LinkedIn-1).
In early 2021, he started computer chess programming [[2]](#cite_note-2) and soon came up with his own [open source chess program](/Category:Open_Source "Category:Open Source") dubbed [MinimalChess](/MinimalChess "MinimalChess") [[3]](#cite_note-3) [[4]](#cite_note-4). In early 2022, he started with a DevLog describing his new project [Leorik](/Leorik "Leorik") [[5]](#cite_note-5).
