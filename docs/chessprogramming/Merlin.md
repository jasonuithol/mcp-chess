**Merlin**,  
a computer chess program developed in the late 70s and early 80s by four students from [Vienna University of Technology](/Vienna_University_of_Technology "Vienna University of Technology"), [Hermann Kaindl](/Hermann_Kaindl "Hermann Kaindl"), [Helmut Horacek](/Helmut_Horacek "Helmut Horacek"), [Marcus Wagner](/Marcus_Wagner "Marcus Wagner") and [Roland Schreier](/index.php?title=Roland_Schreier&action=edit&redlink=1 "Roland Schreier (page does not exist)"), supported by project supervisor [Werner Schimanovich](/Werner_Schimanovich "Werner Schimanovich"). Merlin competed at various [ACM North American Computer Chess Championships](/ACM_North_American_Computer_Chess_Championship "ACM North American Computer Chess Championship") and two [World Computer Chess Championships](/World_Computer_Chess_Championship "World Computer Chess Championship") in the 80s, the [WCCC 1983](/WCCC_1983 "WCCC 1983") in New York and [WCCC 1989](/WCCC_1989 "WCCC 1989") in Edmonton, at the WCCC running on [IBM 370](/IBM_370 "IBM 370") compliant [Siemens 7.890 F](/index.php?title=Siemens-System_7.800&action=edit&redlink=1 "Siemens-System 7.800 (page does not exist)") (1983) and [IBM 3090](/IBM_370 "IBM 370") (1989) [[3]](#cite_note-3). It won the [International Computer Chess Tournament 1984](/International_Computer_Chess_Tournament_1984 "International Computer Chess Tournament 1984") in [Baarn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baarn), [The Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands) [[4]](#cite_note-4).

# Internals

As mentioned in [Werner DePauli-Schimanovich's](/Werner_Schimanovich "Werner Schimanovich") 2006 book *Europolis 6* [[5]](#cite_note-5) and the reprinted paper *Das Schachprogramm Merlin ...* by [Helmut Horacek](/Helmut_Horacek "Helmut Horacek") and [Marcus Wagner](/Marcus_Wagner "Marcus Wagner") [[6]](#cite_note-6), Merlin was initially based on the didactic program [Chess 0.5](/Chess_0.5 "Chess 0.5") by [Larry Atkin](/Larry_Atkin "Larry Atkin") and [Peter W. Frey](/Peter_W._Frey "Peter W. Frey"), as published 1978 in [Byte Magazine](/Byte_Magazine "Byte Magazine"), also written in [Pascal](/Pascal "Pascal") [[7]](#cite_note-7) except some time critical, often called routines, which were re-written in [CDC assembly](/Assembly "Assembly") by Wagner. Merlin was basically a [brute-force](/Brute-Force "Brute-Force") [depth-first](/Depth-First "Depth-First") searcher with knowledge-based [selectivity](/Selectivity "Selectivity") [[8]](#cite_note-8) , using [extensions](/Extensions "Extensions") as well as [null move](/Null_Move "Null Move") to [determine threats](/Null_Move_Pruning#ThreatDetection "Null Move Pruning") [[9]](#cite_note-9) .
