# Hans Zellner

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Hans_Zellner

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* Hans Zellner**

**Hans Zellner**,  
a German programmer involved in the early development and compression of [endgame tablebases](/Endgame_Tablebases "Endgame Tablebases"), making three, four and even five piece [endgames](/Endgame "Endgame") such as [KRK](/KRK "KRK") (July 1985), KQK (September 1985), [KPK](/KPK "KPK") (November 1985), KBBK (July 1986), [KBNK](/KBNK_Endgame "KBNK Endgame"), KQKR, and KBBKN available for [home-computers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_computer), the three piece endgames and KBBK already on a [Commodore 64](/Commodore_64 "Commodore 64") [[1]](#cite_note-1) [[2]](#cite_note-2). He developed a program which perfectly played KQKR to run on an [Atari ST](/Atari_ST "Atari ST") [[3]](#cite_note-3), a five-piece endgame, KBBKN had to be stored on 59 [floppy disks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floppy_disk) [[4]](#cite_note-4).
