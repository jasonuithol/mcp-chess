# PT 45

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/PT_45

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Tournaments](/Tournaments_and_Matches "Tournaments and Matches") \* [CSVN Programmers Tournament](/CSVN_Programmers_Tournament "CSVN Programmers Tournament") \* PT 45**

**[< Prev](/ICT_2013 "ICT 2013") [Next >](/PT_46 "PT 46")**

The **CSVN 45th Programmers Tournament**, superseding the [Dutch Open Computer Chess Championship](/Dutch_Open_Computer_Chess_Championship "Dutch Open Computer Chess Championship") and the [International CSVN Tournament](/International_CSVN_Tournament "International CSVN Tournament"), took place at November 30 and December 01, [2013](/Timeline#2013 "Timeline"), [Denksportcentrum](http://www.denksportcentrumleiden.nl/) [Leiden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leiden).

Twelve programs participated, operated by their respective authors, including the brand new [Deep Fritz](/Fritz "Fritz") by [Gyula Horváth](/Gyula_Horv%C3%A1th "Gyula Horváth"), and [Shredder](/Shredder "Shredder") by [Stefan Meyer-Kahlen](/Stefan_Meyer-Kahlen "Stefan Meyer-Kahlen"). [Jonny](/Jonny "Jonny") by [Johannes Zwanzger](/Johannes_Zwanzger "Johannes Zwanzger"), which played the [WCCC 2013](/WCCC_2013 "WCCC 2013") on 2400 cores of the btrzx3 cluster of the [University of Bayreuth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Bayreuth) [[1]](#cite_note-1) [[2]](#cite_note-2) , used "only" 216 cores but nevertheless won the tournament a half point ahead of its commercial rivals.
