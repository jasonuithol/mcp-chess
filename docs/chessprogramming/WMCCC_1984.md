The **Fourth World Microcomputer Chess Championship** took place from September 9-15, [1984](/Timeline#1984 "Timeline"), at Stakis Ingram Hotel [[1]](#cite_note-1), [Glasgow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glasgow), [Scotland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scotland), [United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom), hosted by the [Scottish Chess Association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chess_Scotland) as part of their centenary celebration. [Mike Valvo](/Michael_Valvo "Michael Valvo") served as Tournament Director, and the [ICCA](/ICCA "ICCA") was represented by [Monroe Newborn](/Monroe_Newborn "Monroe Newborn").

Nineteen programs battled for the title, represented far fewer interests since multiple entries were allowed. Each company was permitted as many as three entries although there was no limit for a particular author, like [Dan](/Dan_Spracklen "Dan Spracklen") and [Kathe Spracklen](/Kathe_Spracklen "Kathe Spracklen") with interests in seven of the nineteen entries. After seven rounds, four programs tied for the first place, presented four different interests which was a mathematical miracle, [Elite X](/Elite "Elite"), [Mephisto III A](/Mephisto_(H)#Mephisto3 "Mephisto (H)"), [Princhess X](/Princhess "Princhess") and [Psion](/Psion "Psion"). The awards ceremony took place in the about-to-be-founded [Turing Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_Institute), the brainchild of [Donald Michie](/Donald_Michie "Donald Michie") and an affiliate of Glasgow's [University of Strathclyde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Strathclyde) [[2]](#cite_note-2).
