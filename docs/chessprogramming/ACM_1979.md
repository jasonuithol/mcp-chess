The [ACM's](/ACM "ACM") **Tenth North American Computer Chess Championship** (NACCC) was held from October 28 to 31, [1979](/Timeline#1979 "Timeline") at the [Detroit Plaza Hotel in the Renaissance Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance_Center), [Detroit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detroit%2C_Michigan), [Michigan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michigan), USA [[2]](#cite_note-2) .In attendance at the tournament as guests of [ACM](/ACM "ACM") were [John McCarthy](/John_McCarthy "John McCarthy") of [Stanford University](/Stanford_University "Stanford University"), [George Koltanowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Koltanowski), former president of the [United States Chess Federation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Chess_Federation) and the world's most famous [blindfold chess](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blindfold_chess) player, and [Max Euwe](/Max_Euwe "Max Euwe"), former president of [FIDE](/FIDE "FIDE") and former World Champion from 1935-1937 [[3]](#cite_note-3) . [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy"), International Master from London, served as Tournament Director. John McCarthy awarded the trophies at a conference luncheon on Wednesday, October 31, and called for placing greater stress on the experimental aspects of the tournament. He encouraged the tournament organizers to require programs to print out more of the important information leading up to their choice of moves. This would permit a clearer understanding of why the computers play as they do. Koltanowski and Euwe mixed with the participants and from time to time came to the stage to assist David Levy. They commented on the games and dipped into their large reservoir of chess related stories and jokes. The tournament was organized by Ira Purchis of the [Burroughs Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burroughs_Corporation), [Ben Mittman](/Ben_Mittman "Ben Mittman"), and [Monroe Newborn](/Monroe_Newborn "Monroe Newborn"). [IBM](/index.php?title=IBM&action=edit&redlink=1 "IBM (page does not exist)"), Burroughs, [Anderson-Jacobson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_SRI_International_spin-offs), [General Motors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Motors), and [Ford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company) provided partial support of the tournament [[4]](#cite_note-4) .

# Exhibition Game

In addition to the chess tournament, ACM 79 was the scene of a most unusual event on October 27, 1979. At the suggestion of [ACM](/ACM "ACM") president [Daniel D. McCracken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_D._McCracken), an exhibition chess game was held between [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy") and a unique team consisting of [Chess 4.9](/Chess_(Program) "Chess (Program)") and [David Slate](/David_Slate "David Slate"). Levy mated advanced Slate in 50 moves [[10]](#cite_note-10) .
