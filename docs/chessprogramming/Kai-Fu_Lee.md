**Kai-Fu Lee**,  
a Taiwanese computer scientist with expertise in [artificial intelligence](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence"), technology executive, [venture capitalist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venture_capital), and writer.
He was founding president of [Google China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_China), serving from July 2005 through September 2009, and was also affiliated with [Microsoft](/Microsoft "Microsoft"), [SGI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_Graphics) and [Apple](/index.php?title=Apple&action=edit&redlink=1 "Apple (page does not exist)").
Kai-Fu Lee graduated from [Columbia University](/Columbia_University "Columbia University") in computer science in 1983, and earned a Ph.D. from [Carnegie Mellon University](/Carnegie_Mellon_University "Carnegie Mellon University") in 1988, where he worked on [machine learning](/Learning "Learning"), [pattern recognition](/Pattern_Recognition "Pattern Recognition") and [speech recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech_recognition), developing the [CMU Sphinx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CMU_Sphinx) system.

# Bill

During the mid and late 80s, along with [Sanjoy Mahajan](/Sanjoy_Mahajan "Sanjoy Mahajan"), Kai-Fu Lee co-authored the at that time world class [Othello](/Othello "Othello") program *Bill* [[2]](#cite_note-2),
initially written in [Common Lisp](/index.php?title=LISP&action=edit&redlink=1 "LISP (page does not exist)") at [Carnegie Mellon University](/Carnegie_Mellon_University "Carnegie Mellon University") in 1985, in 1986 rewritten in [C](/C "C"), and in 2005 released under the [GPL](/Free_Software_Foundation#GPL "Free Software Foundation") [[3]](#cite_note-3).
*Bill 3.0* applies [Bayesian learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayesian_inference) of [discriminant functions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discriminant_function_analysis) that [automatically combines](/Automated_Tuning "Automated Tuning") [evaluation](/Evaluation "Evaluation") features [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5).
