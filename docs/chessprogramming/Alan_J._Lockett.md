Alan J. Lockett [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Alan J. Lockett**,  
an American computer scientist, Ph.D. from [University of Texas at Austin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_Austin) under [Risto Miikkulainen](/Risto_Miikkulainen "Risto Miikkulainen").
In his thesis on [machine learning](/Learning "Learning") and [global optimization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_optimization), he introduced the [martingale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martingale_(probability_theory)) based **Evolutionary Annealing**,
which resembles an [evolutionary algorithms](/Genetic_Programming#EvolutionaryAlgorithms "Genetic Programming") to approximates samples from an increasingly sharp
[Boltzmann distribution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boltzmann_distribution), asymptotically focusing on the global optima.
**Neuroannealing** applies evolutionary annealing to evolve and learn [neural networks](/Neural_Networks "Neural Networks") [[2]](#cite_note-2). His further research interests include [humanoid robotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanoid_robot), [deep learning](/Deep_Learning "Deep Learning") and [opponent modelling](/Opponent_Model_Search "Opponent Model Search") in [games](/Games "Games").
