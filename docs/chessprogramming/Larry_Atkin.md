**Lawrence R. (Larry) Atkin**,  
an American computer scientist, former chess programmer and consultant. Along with [Keith Gorlen](/Keith_Gorlen "Keith Gorlen") (1968), [David Slate](/David_Slate "David Slate") (1969) and [CDC Cyber](/CDC_Cyber "CDC Cyber") hardware consultant [David Cahlander](/David_Cahlander "David Cahlander"), Larry Atkin was the initial author of the [Northwestern University's](/Northwestern_University "Northwestern University") program [Chess](/Chess_(Program) "Chess (Program)"), which almost dominated computer chess during the 70s in the United States. Larry Atkin further was lead programmer at Odesta aka Helix Technologies and co-creator of the [Helix database](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helix_%28database%29) and is now consultant at *QSA Toolworks* [[2]](#cite_note-2) and various companies [[3]](#cite_note-3).

# Chess 0.5

In 1978 Larry Atkin wrote a didactic program in [Pascal](/Pascal "Pascal") along with [Peter W. Frey](/Peter_W._Frey "Peter W. Frey"), which was published as [Chess 0.5](/Chess_0.5 "Chess 0.5") in the [Byte Magazine](/Byte_Magazine "Byte Magazine") [[6]](#cite_note-6), and re-published on-line in 2005, available from [Scott A. Moore's](http://www.moorecad.com/standardpascal/scottmoore.html) sites [[7]](#cite_note-7)[[8]](#cite_note-8).

# Applied Concepts

In the '80s Larry Atkin worked for [Applied Concepts](/Applied_Concepts "Applied Concepts") on [dedicated chess computers](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") and is mentioned along with [David Slate](/David_Slate "David Slate") as co-author of the [Gruenfeld](/Morphy#Gruenfeld "Morphy") and [Capablanca](/Morphy#Capablanca "Morphy") module programs for the [Great Game Machine](/Great_Game_Machine "Great Game Machine") and the [Chafitz modular game system](/Chafitz_Modular_Game_System "Chafitz Modular Game System") . He is further sole author of the [Steinitz Edition 4](/Steinitz#Edition4 "Steinitz") [[9]](#cite_note-9) [[10]](#cite_note-10), and the [Steinitz Encore](/Steinitz#Encore "Steinitz") [[11]](#cite_note-11), both running on a 8-bit [6502](/6502 "6502") processor with 2 resp. 4 Mhz. Capablanca was later converted to be [Chess 7.0](/Chess_7.0 "Chess 7.0") for the [Apple II](/Apple_II "Apple II"), [Atari 800](/Atari_8-bit "Atari 8-bit"), and [Commodore 64](/Commodore_64 "Commodore 64") published by [Odesta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helix_%28database%29). Even later it became "How About a Nice Game of Chess" for the Apple IIe [[12]](#cite_note-12) [[13]](#cite_note-13). Along with Peter W. Frey, Larry Atkin is co-author of the [Othello](/Othello "Othello") program *Odin*, which also ran as module of the [Great Game Machine](/Great_Game_Machine "Great Game Machine").
