**Jonathan Baxter**,  
an Australian computer scientist, entrepreneur, [CEO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_executive_officer) and co-founder of the [Web crawling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_crawler) company *Panscient* [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3), and before quantitative researcher at [Two Sigma Investments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two_Sigma), a [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City) based [hedge fund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hedge_fund), and distinguished research scientist at WhizBang! Labs, [Pittsburgh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pittsburgh), [Pennsylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pennsylvania) [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5) [[6]](#cite_note-6).

# Computer Chess

While affiliated with the Department of Systems Engineering at [Australian National University](/Australian_National_University "Australian National University"), Jonathan Baxter co-authored the [TD-learning](/Temporal_Difference_Learning "Temporal Difference Learning") chess program [KnightCap](/KnightCap "KnightCap") [[7]](#cite_note-7) and further wrote his own engine [TDChess](/TDChess "TDChess") based on KnightCap [[8]](#cite_note-8), which won the [NC3 1999](/NC3_1999 "NC3 1999") [Australasian National Computer Chess Championship](/Australasian_National_Computer_Chess_Championship "Australasian National Computer Chess Championship") [[9]](#cite_note-9).
After the [AlphaZero](/AlphaZero "AlphaZero") incident in December 2017, Jonathan Baxter contributed a [Monte-Carlo Tree Search](/Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search") wrapper for [Stockfish](/Stockfish "Stockfish") [[10]](#cite_note-10).
