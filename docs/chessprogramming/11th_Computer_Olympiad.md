The **Eleventh Computer Olympiad** took place from May 25 to June 1, [2006](/Timeline#2006 "Timeline"), in the [Oval Lingotto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oval_Lingotto), [Turin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turin), [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy), in conjunction with the [Fourteenth World Computer Chess Championship](/WCCC_2006 "WCCC 2006"), the human [FIDE](/FIDE "FIDE") [37th Chess Olympiad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/37th_Chess_Olympiad), and the nearby [5th International Conference on Computers and Games](/CG_2006 "CG 2006") at the [University of Turin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Turin). The [IGCA](/index.php?title=IGCA&action=edit&redlink=1 "IGCA (page does not exist)") events were in the hands of [Paolo Ciancarini](/Paolo_Ciancarini "Paolo Ciancarini") from [University of Bologna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Bologna) as local organizer, supported by the IKAT (Institute for Knowledge and Agent Technology) team from [Maastricht University](/Maastricht_University "Maastricht University"). [Jaap van den Herik](/Jaap_van_den_Herik "Jaap van den Herik") served as tournament director, assisted by [Mark Winands](/Mark_Winands "Mark Winands"). [Fredrik Niemelä](/index.php?title=Fredrik_Niemel%C3%A4&action=edit&redlink=1 "Fredrik Niemelä (page does not exist)") was privileged to lead the [Hex](/Hex "Hex") competition [[2]](#cite_note-2).
