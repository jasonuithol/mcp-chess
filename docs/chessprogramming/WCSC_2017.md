**6th World Chess Software Championship 2017**,  
on July 01 and 02, [2017](/Timeline#2017 "Timeline"), Snellius Building [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3) , [Leiden University](/Leiden_University "Leiden University"), [Leiden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leiden), [The Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands), in conjunction with the [WCCC 2017](/WCCC_2017 "WCCC 2017"), the [Advances in Computer Games 15](/Advances_in_Computer_Games_15 "Advances in Computer Games 15") conference [[4]](#cite_note-4) , and the [20th Computer Olympiad](/20th_Computer_Olympiad "20th Computer Olympiad") [[5]](#cite_note-5). Seven participants, single round robin, 45 minutes per side plus 15 seconds per move.

Hardware for all programs like in the previous two years was [Intel](/Intel "Intel") [i7-3770](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Intel_Core_i7_microprocessors#Ivy_Bridge_microarchitecture_.283rd_generation.29) 3.4GHz 4 cores, 16 GiB [RAM](/Memory#RAM "Memory"). [Shredder](/Shredder "Shredder") by [Stefan Meyer-Kahlen](/Stefan_Meyer-Kahlen "Stefan Meyer-Kahlen") won the World Chess Software Championship the third time in 2017, after already winning the [WCSC 2010](/WCSC_2010 "WCSC 2010") and the [WCSC 2015](/WCSC_2015 "WCSC 2015"), that its the first, fourth and sixth edition of this uniform hardware tournament, and continued its amazing series of tournament victories started back in [1996](/WMCCC_1996 "WMCCC 1996").
