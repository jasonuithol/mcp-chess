Der Greif [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Greif**,  
a chess program by engine author [Michael Ehrig](/Michael_Ehrig "Michael Ehrig"), supported by tester and [opening book author](/Category:Opening_Book_Author "Category:Opening Book Author") [Daniel Mehrmann](/Daniel_Mehrmann "Daniel Mehrmann") [[2]](#cite_note-2).
Greif played the [WMCCC 1993](/WMCCC_1993 "WMCCC 1993") in Munich [[3]](#cite_note-3), the [Aegon 1994](/Aegon_1994 "Aegon 1994") Man-Machine tournament in [The Hague](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hague), and the [IPCCC 1994](/IPCCC_1994 "IPCCC 1994") in [Paderborn](/Paderborn_University "Paderborn University"). At the private [Clodra](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clodra) chess tournament organized by [Rainer Serfling](/Rainer_Serfling "Rainer Serfling") in November 1994,
Greif was recognized as a [clone](/Category:Clone "Category:Clone") of [Chrilly Donninger's](/Chrilly_Donninger "Chrilly Donninger") program [Nimzo 2](/Nimzo "Nimzo") [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5).
