# First International Chess-Computer Tournament in the USSR 1989

The **First International Chess-Computer Tournament in the USSR**,  
held simultaneously with the human qualification tournament of the World Cup, May 16-28, [1989](/Timeline#1989 "Timeline") in [Moscow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moscow), [USSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union), sponsored by [Hegener & Glaser](/Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser"). There was also an exhibition of [Mephisto](/Mephisto "Mephisto") chess computers, with more than 100 machines shown in the *[Izmailovo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Izmaylovo) cinema and concert hall* [[2]](#cite_note-2) to play Soviet chess amateurs. The chess-computer tournament was organized by the [Chess-Programming Association](/First_Soviet_Computer-Chess_Championship_1988#CPA "First Soviet Computer-Chess Championship 1988") (CPA) of the [USSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union). Five Soviet participants, two [PC](/IBM_PC "IBM PC") programs, [Centaur](/Centaur "Centaur") and [Algir](/Algir "Algir"), and three Soviet [dedicated chess computers](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") competed with three Mephisto models with programs by [Richard Lang](/Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") ([Mephisto Almeria](/Mephisto_Almeria "Mephisto Almeria") [[3]](#cite_note-3) ), [Ed Schröder](/Ed_Schroder "Ed Schroder") ([Mephisto München IV](/Mephisto_MM_IV "Mephisto MM IV") [[4]](#cite_note-4) ) and [Frans Morsch](/Frans_Morsch "Frans Morsch") ([Mephisto Europa](/Mephisto_Europa "Mephisto Europa") [[5]](#cite_note-5) ). Finally, the Almeria won the tournament, followed by the MM IV and Centaur.
