**Charlie**,  
a chess program, written in the early 70s by high school student [Andreas Keil](/Andreas_Rudin "Andreas Rudin"), becoming third winner of the German [Jugend forscht](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jugend_forscht) youth science competition 1974, in the category of computer science and mathematics [[2]](#cite_note-2).
Charlie was written in over 6000 lines of [assembly language](/Assembly "Assembly") and LIDIA (Lernen Im DIAlog), a language for [computer-aided teaching](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer-aided#Teaching) [[3]](#cite_note-3). Charlie played the [First GI Computer Chess Tournament](/First_GI_Computer_Chess_Tournament "First GI Computer Chess Tournament") 1975 in [Dortmund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dortmund) [[4]](#cite_note-4), where it ran on a [Siemens 4004](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siemens_2002) [[5]](#cite_note-5). Charlie further played the [First European Computer Chess Championship](/ECCC_1976 "ECCC 1976") 1976 in [Amsterdam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam).
