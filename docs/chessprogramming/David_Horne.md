**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* David Horne**

**David R. Horne**,  
a British inventor [[1]](#cite_note-1), former programmer, and chess programmer. In the early 1980s, David Horne wrote [1K ZX Chess](/1K_ZX_Chess "1K ZX Chess"), one of the world's smallest chess programs, running on the unexpanded [Sinclair ZX81](/Sinclair_ZX81 "Sinclair ZX81") with only one Kib of [RAM](/Memory#RAM "Memory") [[2]](#cite_note-2). 1K ZX Chess was published in [Your Computer](/Your_Computer "Your Computer"), and commercially market by Horne himself [[3]](#cite_note-3) and soon by [Artic Computing](/Artic_Computing "Artic Computing"), also launching Horne's [Chess Tutor](/Chess_Tutor "Chess Tutor") for the [ZX Spectrum](/ZX_Spectrum "ZX Spectrum") in 1983 [[4]](#cite_note-4).
