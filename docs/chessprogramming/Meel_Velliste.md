Meel Velliste [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Meel Velliste**,  
an Estonian computer scientist, biomedical engineer and member of the founding team [[2]](#cite_note-2) at *Fivetran - Integrations for [Amazon Redshift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_Redshift)* [[3]](#cite_note-3), and previously research professor at [University of Pittsburgh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Pittsburgh). He holds a B.Sc. in cybernetics and CS from [University of Reading](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Reading) in 1998, and a Ph.D. in [biomedical engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomedical_engineering) from [Carnegie Mellon University](/Carnegie_Mellon_University "Carnegie Mellon University") in 2002. In Reading, Meel Velliste worked with [Guy Haworth](/Guy_Haworth "Guy Haworth") on [chess endgames](/Endgame "Endgame"), in particular [KPK](/KPK "KPK") and [neural networks](/Neural_Networks "Neural Networks"), as published in the [ICCA Journal](/ICGA_Journal#21_4 "ICGA Journal"), December 1998 [[4]](#cite_note-4).
