Richard Delorme [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Richard Delorme**,  
a French biologist working in the forest management, Ph.D. in human biology from [Claude Bernard University Lyon 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claude_Bernard_University_Lyon_1) in 1997 [[2]](#cite_note-2), software developer, and games and chess programmer. He is author of the strong [Othello](/Othello "Othello") program [Edax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edax_(computing)) written in [C](/C "C") [[3]](#cite_note-3), also analysis engine in [Stephane Nicolet's](/Stephane_Nicolet "Stephane Nicolet") Othello program *Cassio* for [Macintosh](/Macintosh "Macintosh") [[4]](#cite_note-4), and test-bed in [deep learning](/Deep_Learning "Deep Learning") research [[5]](#cite_note-5).
Richard Delorme is furthre author of the [UCI](/UCI "UCI") compliant chess engines [Amoeba](/Amoeba "Amoeba") and its didactic eased counterpart [Dumb](/Dumb "Dumb"), both written in the [D programming language](/D_(Programming_Language) "D (Programming Language)") [[6]](#cite_note-6) [[7]](#cite_note-7).
