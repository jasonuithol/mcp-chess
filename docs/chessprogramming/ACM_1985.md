The [ACM's](/ACM "ACM") **Sixteenth North American Computer Chess Championship** (NACCC) took place from October 13-15, [1985](/Timeline#1985 "Timeline"), Radisson Hotel [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3) [[4]](#cite_note-4) , [Denver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denver%2C_Colorado), [Colorado](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colorado), USA. Special purpose hardware chess entity [HiTech](/HiTech "HiTech") by a team from [Carnegie Mellon University](/Carnegie_Mellon_University "Carnegie Mellon University") headed by [Hans Berliner](/Hans_Berliner "Hans Berliner") won its first official computer chess tournament conveniently with a perfect score of 4 out of 4, winning from [Lachex](/Lachex "Lachex"), [Phoenix](/Phoenix "Phoenix"), [Bebe](/Bebe "Bebe") and [Cray Blitz](/Cray_Blitz "Cray Blitz").

## Aftermath

A few months later, during the [WCCC 1986](/WCCC_1986 "WCCC 1986") in [Cologne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cologne) after the loss in round 2 from [Bobby](/Bobby "Bobby"), [Robert Hyatt](/Robert_Hyatt "Robert Hyatt") found some suspicious [pawn hole](/Holes "Holes") code in Cray Blitz, already implemented in summer 1985 after test games versus a [Super Constellation](/Super_Constellation "Super Constellation"), scoring better against that particular opponent, but apparently causing Cray Blitz to play passively against tactical stronger programs. After removing the few lines of pawn hole code, Cray Blitz could take revenge versus [Bebe](/Bebe "Bebe") and finally [HiTech](/HiTech "HiTech") to defend its title [[11]](#cite_note-11) .
