# Intelligent Software

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Intelligent_Software

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Organizations](/Organizations "Organizations") \* Intelligent Software**

**Intelligent Software**,  
a British computer chess and AI games developing and trading company with focus on [research and development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research_and_development) of [dedicated chess computers](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers"), founded in autumn 1981 by [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy") and [Kevin O’Connell](/Kevin_O%E2%80%99Connell "Kevin O’Connell"). Intelligent Software evolved from their former company [Philidor Software](/Philidor_Software "Philidor Software"), initially as a separate company to no longer develop programs exclusively for [SciSys](/Saitek "Saitek"), but also for [Milton Bradley](/Milton_Bradley "Milton Bradley"), [Newcrest Technology](/Newcrest_Technology "Newcrest Technology"), and others [[1]](#cite_note-1) [[2]](#cite_note-2) . The new company name was already advised by *Intelligent Games Ltd.*, distributing [Intelligent Chess](/Intelligent_Chess "Intelligent Chess") since 1980. Intelligent Software had several computer chess programmers under contract. The programmers already affiliated with Philidor Software, [Mark Taylor](/Mark_Taylor "Mark Taylor"), [David Broughton](/David_Broughton "David Broughton"), and [Mike Johnson](/Mike_Johnson "Mike Johnson"), were supplemented by [Richard Lang](/Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") and a [Checkers](/Checkers "Checkers") programmer called [Martin Bryant](/Martin_Bryant "Martin Bryant") [[3]](#cite_note-3) .

# Enterprise

In late 1982, Intelligent Software was commissioned to develop a [home computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_computer), the [Z80](/Z80 "Z80") based [Enterprise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enterprise_%28computer%29) with 64 resp. 128 KiB of [bank switched](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bank_switching) [RAM](/Memory#RAM "Memory"), and a [Basic](/Basic "Basic") interpreter in [ROM](/Memory#ROM "Memory"). Announced in September 1983 with a advised release date of April 1984, the Enterprise did not ship until 1985 [[4]](#cite_note-4) . In Germany, the computer was distributed by [Hegener & Glaser](/Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser") as *Mephisto PHC 64*.

# Close Down

Intelligent Software was closed down in 1986 when its biggest customer, the commissioning company defaulted on a very large payment [[5]](#cite_note-5) .
