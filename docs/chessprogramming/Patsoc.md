**Patsoc**,  
a computer chess program by [Hans Berliner](/Hans_Berliner "Hans Berliner") written in [BLISS 36](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BLISS) [[1]](#cite_note-1), which played the [WCCC 1983](/WCCC_1983 "WCCC 1983") in [New York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City), running on a [DEC](/Digital_Equipment_Corporation "Digital Equipment Corporation") [PDP-10](/PDP-10 "PDP-10") with [36 bit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/36-bit) word length. Patsoc first implemented the evaluation feature of the [crippled majority](/Doubled_Pawn#CrippledMajority "Doubled Pawn") with [doubled pawns](/Doubled_Pawn "Doubled Pawn") involved [[2]](#cite_note-2). Nevertheless, Patsoc is the acronym of "Plays a terrible sort of Chess" [[3]](#cite_note-3).
