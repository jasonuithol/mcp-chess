Chess 232 [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Chess 232 Board**,  
a magnetic sensory board with wooden pieces to play with [PC](/IBM_PC "IBM PC") chess programs, first released in late 1994, deprecated and out of stock [[2]](#cite_note-2). The board was connected with the PC via a [RS-323](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RS-232) [serial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serial_port) [COM port](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COM_%28hardware_interface%29). It was developed in conjunction with his [Auto232](/Auto232 "Auto232") interface, by [Chrilly Donninger](/Chrilly_Donninger "Chrilly Donninger"), using the same protocol [[3]](#cite_note-3) [[4]](#cite_note-4). A [MS-DOS](/MS-DOS "MS-DOS") [TSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terminate_and_stay_resident_program) driver was able to "hook" the input and output of various [chess programs](/MS-DOS#Engines "MS-DOS"). A later free [Windows](/Windows "Windows") driver by [Stefan Meyer-Kahlen](/Stefan_Meyer-Kahlen "Stefan Meyer-Kahlen") allowed dedicated Chess 232 feeling with Windows engines [[5]](#cite_note-5).
