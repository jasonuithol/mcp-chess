Xinbo Gao [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Xinbo Gao**,  
a Chinese electrical engineer, computer scientist, and full professor and director of the Video and Image Processing System (VIPS) Laboratory, School of Electronic Engineering, [Xidian University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xidian_University), [Xi'an](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi%27an), [Shaanxi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shaanxi) Province.
He holds a Bachelor in [electronic engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_engineering), and Master and Ph.D. degrees in [signal processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Signal_processing) and [information processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_processing) from Xidian University in 1994, 1997 and 1999, respectively.
His research interests include [visual information perception](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visual_perception), [pattern recognition](/Pattern_Recognition "Pattern Recognition"), [Fuzzy clustering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuzzy_clustering), [machine learning](/Learning "Learning"), [computational intelligence](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") [[2]](#cite_note-2), and applying [deep learning](/Deep_Learning "Deep Learning") with [convolutional neural networks](/Neural_Networks#Convolutional "Neural Networks") to [facial recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facial_recognition_system).

# (D, d)-OM Search

In the late 90s, Xinbo Gao was research fellow in [Hiroyuki Iida's](/Hiroyuki_Iida "Hiroyuki Iida") group at [Shizuoka University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shizuoka_University), [Hamamatsu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamamatsu,_Shizuoka), [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan), where he worked along with Hiroyuki Iida, [Jos Uiterwijk](/Jos_Uiterwijk "Jos Uiterwijk"), and [Jaap van den Herik](/Jaap_van_den_Herik "Jaap van den Herik") on a generalization of [opponent model search](/Opponent_Model_Search "Opponent Model Search"), called **(D, d)-OM** search, where **D** stands for the [depth](/Depth "Depth") of search by the player and **d** for the opponent’s depth of search [[3]](#cite_note-3).
