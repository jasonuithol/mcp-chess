# EGA Chess

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/EGA_Chess

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Engines](/Engines "Engines") \* EGA Chess**

**EGA Chess**,  
an early chess program for the [IBM PC](/IBM_PC "IBM PC") by [Lloyd L. Lank](/Lloyd_L._Lank "Lloyd L. Lank"), featuring a [graphics board](/2D_Graphics_Board "2D Graphics Board") using the [EGA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enhanced_Graphics_Adapter) or [VGA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_Graphics_Array) display standards, running under [DOS 2.0](/MS-DOS "MS-DOS") or later, commercially released in 1988 by *Cube Microsystems*, [Overland Park, Kansas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overland_Park,_Kansas) [[1]](#cite_note-1). The game had an on-line tutorial that include animated explanations of moves such as [castling](/Castling "Castling") and [en passant](/En_passant "En passant").

# Tournament Play

EGA Chess played the [Second United States Open Computer Chess Championship 1986](/USOCCC_1986 "USOCCC 1986") [[2]](#cite_note-2), the [Third United States Open Computer Chess Championship 1987](/USOCCC_1987 "USOCCC 1987") [[3]](#cite_note-3) and the [Fourth United States Open Computer Chess Championship 1988](/USOCCC_1988 "USOCCC 1988") [[4]](#cite_note-4).
