# Crazyhouse

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Crazyhouse

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Games](/Games "Games") \* Crazyhouse**

**Crazyhouse**,  
a [chess variant](/Chess#Variants "Chess") which incorporates the [Shogi](/Shogi "Shogi") rule in which a player can [drop](/index.php?title=Piece_Drop&action=edit&redlink=1 "Piece Drop (page does not exist)") a [captured piece](/Captures "Captures"). A captured piece reverses its color and goes to the capturing player's reserve or pocket. At any time, instead of making a move with a piece on the board, a player can drop a piece from his reserve onto an empty square on the board [[1]](#cite_note-1).

Crazyhouse could be played with [Zillions of Games](/Zillions_of_Games "Zillions of Games") by [Jeff Mallett](/Jeff_Mallett "Jeff Mallett") and [Mark Lefler](/Mark_Lefler "Mark Lefler"). A pioneer in implementing [Bughouse](/index.php?title=Bughouse&action=edit&redlink=1 "Bughouse (page does not exist)") and Crazyhouse with his engine [Sunsetter](/Sunsetter "Sunsetter") was [Georg von Zimmermann](/Georg_von_Zimmermann "Georg von Zimmermann"). More recently, [Harm Geert Muller](/Harm_Geert_Muller "Harm Geert Muller") made the necessary extensions and generalizations of the [Chess Engine Communication Protocol](/Chess_Engine_Communication_Protocol "Chess Engine Communication Protocol") along with its [XBoard](/XBoard "XBoard") and [WinBoard](/WinBoard "WinBoard") [GUIs](/GUI "GUI") to support Crazyhouse as well as other variants and games [[2]](#cite_note-2) , and also published his new engine [CrazyWa](/index.php?title=CrazyWa&action=edit&redlink=1 "CrazyWa (page does not exist)") which supports Crazyhouse plus five Shogi variants [[3]](#cite_note-3). [Multi Variant Stockfish](/index.php?title=Multi_Variant_Stockfish&action=edit&redlink=1 "Multi Variant Stockfish (page does not exist)"), a [Stockfish](/Stockfish "Stockfish") fork maintained by [Daniel Dugovic](/index.php?title=Daniel_Dugovic&action=edit&redlink=1 "Daniel Dugovic (page does not exist)") [[4]](#cite_note-4) [[5]](#cite_note-5), incorporated in various [Lichess](/index.php?title=Lichess&action=edit&redlink=1 "Lichess (page does not exist)") projects [[6]](#cite_note-6) [[7]](#cite_note-7), is able to play Crazyhouse as well. In 8 months, Stockfish-Crazyhouse was improved by 800 Elo points [[8]](#cite_note-8). In 2016, [Ferdinand Mosca](/Ferdinand_Mosca "Ferdinand Mosca") started to play Crazyhouse tournaments [[9]](#cite_note-9) to maintain a rating lists [[10]](#cite_note-10).
