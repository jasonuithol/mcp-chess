Hotel Playadulce [[1]](#cite_note-1)

The **Eighth World Microcomputer Chess Championship** took place from September 24 to October 1, [1988](/Timeline#1988 "Timeline"), Hotel Playadulce, [Aguadulce](http://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aguadulce_%28Almer%C3%ADa%29) near [Almería](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almer%C3%ADa), [Province of Almería](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Province_of_Almer%C3%ADa), [Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain). Just over one week before the scheduled starting date tournament director [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy") was told that the main sponsor had pulled out. Spanish computer chess organizer [Amador Cuesta](/index.php?title=Amador_Cuesta&action=edit&redlink=1 "Amador Cuesta (page does not exist)") tried in vain to find alternative sponsorship and then resigned from the organizing committee with only two days to go. David Levy managed to find a last minute sponsor to cover the essential costs and it was possible to get the tournament off the ground on time. In the end everyone had an enjoyable time and the idea of a seaside venue proved extremely popular [[2]](#cite_note-2). All three groups played were dominated by [Richard Lang's](/Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") [Mephisto Almeria](/Mephisto_Almeria "Mephisto Almeria") programs.

## Pandix - Y!88

[Pandix](/Pandix "Pandix") by [Gyula Horváth](/Gyula_Horv%C3%A1th "Gyula Horváth") won the extra title of the *World PC Champion* [[6]](#cite_note-6). In a notable game Pandix played the [color reverse](/Color_Flipping#BookTransposition "Color Flipping") [Ruy Lopez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruy_Lopez) versus [Y!88](/Y! "Y!") by 1. e3 e5 2. e4 which took [Why Not 88](/Y! "Y!") out of book, while Pandix was able to play along a reversed book line [[7]](#cite_note-7):
