# José Antônio Fabiano Mendes

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/José_Antônio_Fabiano_Mendes

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* José Antônio Fabiano Mendes**

**José Antônio Fabiano Mendes**,  
a Brazilian mathematician and historian of mathematics with interests in computer science, chess and computer chess.
He contributed to [Tim Krabbé's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tim_Krabb%C3%A9) chess records [[1]](#cite_note-1),
[Edward Winter's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Winter_%28chess_historian%29) Chess Seesaw [[2]](#cite_note-2),
and is mentioned on various sites related to puzzles [[3]](#cite_note-3) and mathematics. José was frequent poster in [CCC](/CCC "CCC") in the early 2000s with focus on [test-positions](/Test-Positions "Test-Positions") and [studies](/Chess_Problems,_Compositions_and_Studies "Chess Problems, Compositions and Studies").
