Erik van der Werf [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Erik van der Werf**,  
a Dutch computer scientist, principal scientist in the [hearing aid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_aid) industry, and master level [Go](/Go "Go") player [[2]](#cite_note-2).
He received a M.Sc. degree in [applied physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_physics) from [Delft University of Technology](/Delft_University_of_Technology "Delft University of Technology") in 1999, and defended his Ph.D. thesis on [artificial intelligence](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") for the game of [Go](/Go "Go") at [Maastricht University](/Maastricht_University "Maastricht University") in 2005 under supervision of [Jaap van den Herik](/Jaap_van_den_Herik "Jaap van den Herik") and [Jos Uiterwijk](/Jos_Uiterwijk "Jos Uiterwijk").
Erik van der Werf is author of the [Go](/Go "Go") playing programs [Magog](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=137) (started with [Levente Kocsis](/Levente_Kocsis "Levente Kocsis") and [Mark Winands](/Mark_Winands "Mark Winands")) [[3]](#cite_note-3), [Steenvreter](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=519) [[4]](#cite_note-4) and GridMaster [[5]](#cite_note-5).
His Mini Go Solver *Migos* solved 5x5 Go in 2002 [[6]](#cite_note-6),
using [iterative deepening](/Iterative_Deepening "Iterative Deepening") [alpha-beta](/Alpha-Beta "Alpha-Beta") search ([PVS](/Principal_Variation_Search "Principal Variation Search")), with [transposition tables](/Transposition_Table "Transposition Table"), [enhanced transposition cut-offs](/Enhanced_Transposition_Cutoff "Enhanced Transposition Cutoff"),
two [killer moves](/Killer_Move "Killer Move"), [history heuristic](/History_Heuristic "History Heuristic"), Benson's algorithm for unconditional [live](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_and_death) [[7]](#cite_note-7) [[8]](#cite_note-8), and domain specific evaluation heuristics, futher elaborated in his Ph.D. thesis and [ICGA Journal](/ICGA_Journal "ICGA Journal") papers.
