**Munjong Kolss**,  
a Korean born, German biologist, chess and [correspondence chess](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Correspondence_chess) player [[2]](#cite_note-2) and computer chess programmer. He graduated in biology at [University of Kiel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Kiel) in 2003, received a Ph.D from [University of Fribourg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Fribourg), Switzerland, in 2007, was postdoc at [Bielefeld University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bielefeld_University) [[3]](#cite_note-3), and is now deputy head of the department for evaluations, [University of Cologne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Cologne) [[4]](#cite_note-4).
Along with his twin brother [Muntsin](/Muntsin_Kolss "Muntsin Kolss"), Munjong is author of the chess engine [Ikarus](/Ikarus "Ikarus").
