A Nonogram puzzle [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Nonogram**,  
a [logic puzzle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logic_puzzle) in which cells in a grid must be painted according to the given row [clues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clue) and column clues. Players are requested to paint each grid cell into either “white” or “black”, such that the segments constituting of consecutive black cells in each row or column matches the corresponding row clue and column clue. The clues or numbers are a form of [discrete tomography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discrete_tomography) that measures how many unbroken lines of filled-in squares there are in any given row or column. Solving nonogram puzzles is a [NP-complete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NP-complete) problem [[2]](#cite_note-2) .
