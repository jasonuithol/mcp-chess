The [ACM's](/ACM "ACM") **Twelfth North American Computer Chess Championship** (NACCC) was held from November 8 to 11, [1981](/Timeline#1981 "Timeline") at the [Bonaventure Hotel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westin_Bonaventure_Hotel), [Los Angeles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles%2C_California), [California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California), USA [[2]](#cite_note-2) .

[Mike Valvo](/Michael_Valvo "Michael Valvo") acted as Tournament Director, replacing [David Levy](/David_Levy "David Levy"), who previously served in that capacity and was expected to enter his [own program](/Philidor "Philidor"). Tournament guests were *Shelby Lyman* [[3]](#cite_note-3), chess commentator for [WNET's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WNET) television chess shows and [emcee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_of_ceremonies) for the [Fisher-Spassky World Championship Match](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Chess_Championship_1972) in 1972 [[4]](#cite_note-4) , and [Isaac Kashdan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isaac_Kashdan), Chess Editor for the [Los Angeles Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times). A computer chess panel discussion and short paper presentation was part of the technical program [[5]](#cite_note-5) .
