John Poduska [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**John William Poduska Jr.**,  
an American computer scientist, and as former computer chess programmer author of the chess program [Chaturanga](/Chaturanga "Chaturanga"), which played the [ACM 1981](/ACM_1981 "ACM 1981") [[2]](#cite_note-2) and [ACM 1982](/ACM_1982 "ACM 1982") [[3]](#cite_note-3).
John Poduska Jr. is son of [John William Poduska, Sr.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Poduska), a technological entrepreneur, independent business consultant and in 1979 the founder of [Apollo Computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apollo_Computer), which was one of the first creators of graphical [workstations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Workstation) in the 1980s [[4]](#cite_note-4).
Therefore [Chaturanga](/Chaturanga "Chaturanga") was obligated to run on John Poduska's father's hardware.
