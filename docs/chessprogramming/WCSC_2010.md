Shiinoki Cultural Complex [[1]](#cite_note-1)

The **First World Chess Software Championship** took place from September 24 to October 2, [2010](/Timeline#2010 "Timeline"), at Shiinoki Cultural Complex, [Kanazawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanazawa,_Ishikawa), [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan), in conjunction with the [18th World Computer Chess Championship](/WCCC_2010 "WCCC 2010"), the [15th Computer Olympiad](/15th_Computer_Olympiad "15th Computer Olympiad") and the [7th Computer and Games](/CG_2010 "CG 2010") conference. All events were hosted by [Japan Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (JAIST)](/JAIST "JAIST") and organized by the [ICGA](/ICGA "ICGA") [[2]](#cite_note-2). Unified Hardware was based on [Intel® Xeon® W3520, 8M Cache, 2.66 GHz](http://ark.intel.com/Product.aspx?id=39718), Quad-Core, [Hyper-threading](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyper-threading) off [[3]](#cite_note-3). The winner of the 2010 WCSC is [Shredder](/Shredder "Shredder"), [Rondo](/Rondo "Rondo") runner up a half point behind, and bronze for [Thinker](/Thinker "Thinker").

# Time control

The time limit for the WCSC will be such as to aim for a 2-hour playing session, namely all moves in 45 minutes plus an increment of 15 seconds per move. [[6]](#cite_note-6) .
