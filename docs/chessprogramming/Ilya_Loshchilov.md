Ilya Loshchilov [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Ilya Gennadyevich Loshchilov**,  
a Russian computer scientist in the field of [evolutionary algorithms](/Genetic_Programming#EvolutionaryAlgorithms "Genetic Programming") (EA), [optimization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_optimization) and [machine learning](/Learning "Learning").
He worked with [Marc Schoenauer](/Marc_Schoenauer "Marc Schoenauer") and [Michèle Sebag](/Mich%C3%A8le_Sebag "Michèle Sebag") at [Paris-Sud 11 University](/University_of_Paris#11 "University of Paris") and [INRIA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Institute_for_Research_in_Computer_Science_and_Automation) within their *TAO Project Team* [[2]](#cite_note-2) on [multi-objective optimization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-objective_optimization),
[adaptive coordinate descent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adaptive_coordinate_descent) and [CMA-ES](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CMA-ES), where he defended his Ph.D. with the title *Surrogate-Assisted Evolutionary Algorithms* in 2013 [[3]](#cite_note-3).
In his thesis, he acknowledged *Leonid Ivanovich Babak* [[4]](#cite_note-4) from [Tomsk State University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tomsk_State_University) as his first scientific advisor who showed him the power of evolution, and his chess teacher *Vladimir Andreevich Zhukov* who showed him that there is the pleasure of thinking. As postdoc at [University of Freiburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Freiburg), he continued his research with [Frank Hutter](/Frank_Hutter "Frank Hutter"), combining evolutionary algorithms with [deep learning](/Deep_Learning "Deep Learning") techniques.

# SGDR

Loshchilov's and Hutter's *[stochastic gradient descent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stochastic_gradient_descent) with warm restarts* (**SGDR**) as introduced in their 2016 paper [[5]](#cite_note-5), is mentioned as method for [learning rate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Learning_rate) scheduling to train [Leela Chess'](/Leela_Chess_Zero "Leela Chess Zero") third party network **Leelenstein** [[6]](#cite_note-6), which is combined with [Allie](/Allie "Allie") for the [AllieStein](/Allie#AllieStein "Allie") chess playing entity.
