# Eli David

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Eli_David

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* Eli David**

**Eli (Omid) David**,  
a Ph.D. in computer science, and the author of chess engines [Genesis](/Genesis_IL "Genesis IL") and [Falcon](/Falcon "Falcon") (which participated in several [World Computer Chess Championships](/World_Computer_Chess_Championship "World Computer Chess Championship")). His main research areas are [evolutionary computation](/Genetic_Programming#EvolutionaryComputation "Genetic Programming"), [machine learning](/Learning "Learning"), and computer games. Omid's Ph.D. thesis focused on applying [genetic algorithms](/Genetic_Programming "Genetic Programming") for automatically [learning](/Learning "Learning") the parameters of a chess program's [evaluation function](/Evaluation "Evaluation") and [selective](/Selectivity "Selectivity") [search](/Search "Search"). His paper titled *Genetic Algorithms for Mentor-Assisted Evaluation Function Optimization was* the winner of *GECCO 2008* Best Paper Award in Real-World Applications track. Several of his earlier computer chess papers are on [selective search](/Pruning "Pruning"), and especially variations on [null-move pruning](/Null_Move_Pruning "Null Move Pruning"). In 2016, he was awarded best paper in *ICAAN 2016* for *DeepChess: End-to-End Deep Neural Network for Automatic Learning in Chess* [[1]](#cite_note-1) [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3).
