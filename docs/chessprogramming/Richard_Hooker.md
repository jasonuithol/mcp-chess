**Richard Hooker**,  
a British computer scientist and games programmer. Along with [Tony Guilfoyle](/Tony_Guilfoyle "Tony Guilfoyle"), Richard Hooker is co-author of the chess program [Vaxchess](/Vaxchess "Vaxchess") and the [Scrabble](/index.php?title=Scrabble&action=edit&redlink=1 "Scrabble (page does not exist)") program *Quetzal*, which is based on [Monte-Carlo sampling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monte_Carlo_method), and won the Gold medal at the [4th Computer Olympiad, Scrabble](/4th_Computer_Olympiad#Scrabble "4th Computer Olympiad"), London, 1992 [[1]](#cite_note-1).
