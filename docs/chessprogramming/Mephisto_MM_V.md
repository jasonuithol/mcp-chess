Mephisto MM V [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Mephisto MM V**,  
a [dedicated chess computer](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") [module](/Module "Module") for [Mephisto module systems](/Mephisto_Module_Systems "Mephisto Module Systems") by [Hegener & Glaser](/Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser") launched in 1990 as successor of the [Mephisto MM IV](/Mephisto_MM_IV "Mephisto MM IV") with the same hardware. [Ed Schröder's](/Ed_Schroder "Ed Schroder") [Rebel](/Rebel "Rebel") program ran on a [65C02](/6502 "6502") [CMOS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CMOS) processor in 32 KiB [ROM](/Memory#ROM "Memory") with 8 KiB [RAM](/Memory#RAM "Memory") at 5 MHz, but gained 70 Elo over its predecessor and was [tactically](/Tactics "Tactics") much stronger since it could solve deep [combinations](/Combination "Combination"), while only doing 500-600 [nodes per second](/Nodes_per_Second "Nodes per Second") [[2]](#cite_note-2), due to searching all check giving moves in the first at least two [plies](/Ply "Ply") of the [quiescence search](/Quiescence_Search "Quiescence Search"), and even deeper if check evasion had only one or two legal moves [[3]](#cite_note-3).
