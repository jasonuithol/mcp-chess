**The Fifth World Microcomputer Chess Championship** took place from September 7-15, [1985](/Timeline#1985 "Timeline"), at the [World Trade Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Trade_Center_%28Amsterdam%29) located at the [Zuidas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zuidas) in [Amsterdam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam), the [Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands). Despite a field of 16 chess computers in the commercial group, there were six participants with up to three entries each. A 32-bit version of [Richard Lang's](/Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") [Mephisto Amsterdam](/Mephisto_Amsterdam "Mephisto Amsterdam") won conveniently, followed by two 16-bit versions of the same program [[2]](#cite_note-2) . Additionaly, there was an amateur group with five participants. The title of the *Amateur World Microcomputer Chess Champion* was confidently won by [Frans Morsch's](/Frans_Morsch "Frans Morsch") program [Nona](/Nona "Nona") with a 100% score.

# Special Event

During the Championship, IGM [Gennadi Sosonko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gennadi_Sosonko) did a simultaneous exhibition on September 12-13, from 7:15pm to 3:15am (8 hours!) against 21 participating and 10 other chess computers. Score: 25.5 - 5.5 (82.3%) [[4]](#cite_note-4) .
