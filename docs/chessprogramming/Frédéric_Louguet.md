**Frédéric Louguet**,  
a French computer chess programmer, journalist and writer, in 2006 co-founder and now [CTO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_technology_officer) of *Ranch Computing*, a [Render farm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Render_farm) located in [Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris) with huge [GPU](/GPU "GPU") resources [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3).
Frédéric Louguet is author of the chess programs [Chess Wizard](/Chess_Wizard "Chess Wizard"), three time winner of the [French Computer Chess Championships](/French_Computer_Chess_Championship "French Computer Chess Championship"),
and the experimental program [Raptor](/Raptor "Raptor") [[4]](#cite_note-4).
Frédéric Louguet was editor of the French computer chess magazine [La Puce Echiquéenne](/La_Puce_Echiqu%C3%A9enne "La Puce Echiquéenne"), which appeared in 17 issues from 1992 until 1997 [[5]](#cite_note-5), author of [LCT II](/LCT_II "LCT II"), the **Louguet Chess Test II** [[6]](#cite_note-6) and author of books about various computer related issues.
