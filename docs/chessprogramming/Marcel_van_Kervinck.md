Marcel van Kervinck [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Marcel van Kervinck**, (March 27, 1972 – May 25, 2020 [[2]](#cite_note-2))  
was a Dutch software engineer and [metrology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metrology). Marcel was author of the chess programs [Rookie](/Rookie "Rookie") [[3]](#cite_note-3), [MSCP](/MSCP "MSCP") (Marcel’s Simple Chess Program) [[4]](#cite_note-4), and the [UCI](/UCI "UCI") engine [Floyd](/Floyd "Floyd") for study purposes [[5]](#cite_note-5), author of the online [opening](/Opening "Opening") database [Bookie](/index.php?title=Bookie&action=edit&redlink=1 "Bookie (page does not exist)") [[6]](#cite_note-6), and co-author of the [Gigatron](/Hardware#Gigatron "Hardware") retro-style 8-bit microcomputer [[7]](#cite_note-7) [[8]](#cite_note-8). From December 2012 until November 2014, he was a member of the board of the [CSVN](/CSVN "CSVN").
Marcel van Kervinck passed away on May 25, 2020 [[9]](#cite_note-9).
