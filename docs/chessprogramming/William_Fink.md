**William A. Fink**,  
an American early microcomputer chess programmer and author of the commercial chess program [Sfinks](/Sfinks "Sfinks") [[1]](#cite_note-1), written in [Z80](/Z80 "Z80")-[assembly](/Assembly "Assembly") for a [TRS-80](/TRS-80 "TRS-80") [microcomputer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microcomputer), later ported to [8086](/8086 "8086") for the [IBM PC](/IBM_PC "IBM PC").

# Enhancement

In his 1982 [ICCA Newsletter](/ICGA_Journal#5_1 "ICGA Journal") article *An Enhancement to the Iterative, Alpha-Beta, Minimax Search Procedure* [[2]](#cite_note-2), William Fink describes the implementation of a small [refutation table](/Refutation_Table "Refutation Table") inside his [iterative](/Iterative_Deepening "Iterative Deepening") [alpha-beta](/Alpha-Beta "Alpha-Beta") framework - to keep two [moves](/Moves "Moves") of the [variation](/Principal_Variation "Principal Variation") for each [root](/Root "Root") move, and to search those moves first accordingly during the next iteration.
