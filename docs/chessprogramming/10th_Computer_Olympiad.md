Institute of Information Science [[1]](#cite_note-1)

The **Tenth Computer Olympiad** took place from September 3 to 6, [2005](/Timeline#2005 "Timeline") at Institute of Information Science, [Academia Sinica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academia_Sinica), [Taipei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taipei), [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan), in conjunction with the [Advances in Computer Games 11](/Advances_in_Computer_Games_11 "Advances in Computer Games 11") conference [[2]](#cite_note-2). The organization of both events by the [ICGA](/ICGA "ICGA") was in the hands of Academia Sinica, in particular [Tsan-sheng Hsu](/Tsan-sheng_Hsu "Tsan-sheng Hsu") and his staff, supported by the IKAT (Institute for Knowledge and Agent Technology) team from [Maastricht University](/Maastricht_University "Maastricht University"). [Jaap van den Herik](/Jaap_van_den_Herik "Jaap van den Herik") served as tournament director, assisted by Tsan-sheng Hsu. The [Chinese Chess](/Chinese_Chess "Chinese Chess") tournament with a strong field of 14 participants was led by [Tony Marsland](/Tony_Marsland "Tony Marsland") [[3]](#cite_note-3).

The 10th Computer Olympiad was the first one not directly associated with a (Western) computer chess event, typically the [World Computer Chess Championship](/World_Computer_Chess_Championship "World Computer Chess Championship") - the [WCCC 2005](/WCCC_2005 "WCCC 2005") already took place from August 13 to 21 in [Reykjavík](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reykjav%C3%ADk), [Iceland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iceland).
