**Gian-Carlo Pascutto**, (GCP)  
a Belgian [[2]](#cite_note-2) computer scientist, and computer chess and [Go](/Go "Go") programmer with further interests in [sound recording and reproduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction) and [digital signal processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_signal_processing) [[3]](#cite_note-3). Gian-Carlo is author of the free [open source](/Category:Open_Source "Category:Open Source") chess and [chess variants](/Chess#Variants "Chess") engine [Sjeng](/Sjeng "Sjeng") [[4]](#cite_note-4), the chess engine [Deep Sjeng](/Deep_Sjeng "Deep Sjeng"), which emerged from the closed source Sjeng 12.7 branch [[5]](#cite_note-5) since 2003 commercial [[6]](#cite_note-6), the experimental chess engine [Stoofvlees](/Stoofvlees "Stoofvlees"), and the Go playing program [Leela](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=531) [[7]](#cite_note-7).

# Leela Zero

More recently, after [DeepMind](/index.php?title=DeepMind&action=edit&redlink=1 "DeepMind (page does not exist)") published the [AlphaGo Zero](/index.php?title=AlphaGo&action=edit&redlink=1 "AlphaGo (page does not exist)") paper [[8]](#cite_note-8), Gian-Carlo Pascutto designed and initiated [Leela Zero](/index.php?title=Leela_Zero&action=edit&redlink=1 "Leela Zero (page does not exist)") [[9]](#cite_note-9), a Go playing entity using [MCTS](/Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search") (but without Monte Carlo playouts) and a [deep](/Neural_Networks#Deep "Neural Networks") [residual](/Neural_Networks#Residual "Neural Networks") [convolutional neural network](/Neural_Networks#Convolutional "Neural Networks") stack, trained by a public, distributed effort, as [Leela Chess Zero](/Leela_Chess_Zero "Leela Chess Zero") also adapted to chess [[10]](#cite_note-10) [[11]](#cite_note-11).
