[Pelias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pelias), [Tyro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyro) and [Neleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neleus) [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Tyro**,  
a chess program by [Albert Zobrist](/Albert_Zobrist "Albert Zobrist") and [Frederic Roy Carlson](/Frederic_Roy_Carlson "Frederic Roy Carlson"), affiliated with the [University of Southern California](/University_of_Southern_California "University of Southern California") and the [Jet Propulsion Laboratory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jet_Propulsion_Laboratory). Tyro was successor of [USC CP](/USC_CP "USC CP") [[2]](#cite_note-2) and participated at three [ACM North American Computer Chess Championships](/ACM_North_American_Computer_Chess_Championship "ACM North American Computer Chess Championship"), [ACM 1974](/ACM_1974 "ACM 1974"), [ACM 1975](/ACM_1975 "ACM 1975"), and at [ACM 1977](/ACM_1977 "ACM 1977") running on a [PDP-10 KL10](/PDP-10 "PDP-10") [[3]](#cite_note-3).
