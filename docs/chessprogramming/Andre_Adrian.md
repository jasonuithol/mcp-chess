# Andre Adrian

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Andre_Adrian

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* Andre Adrian**

**Andre Adrian**,  
a German senior engineer with background in [telecommunication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telecommunication), [real-time processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real-time_computing) and [air traffic control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_traffic_control). His interests include computer chess and computer chess history, he maintains a computer chess site in German, covering [El Ajedrecista](/El_Ajedrecista "El Ajedrecista"), [Turochamp](/Turochamp "Turochamp"), [Dietrich Prinz'](/Dietrich_Prinz "Dietrich Prinz") [Mate-in-two](/Mate-in-two "Mate-in-two"), and early chess programs and [Dedicated Chess Computers](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") [[1]](#cite_note-1). Andre Adrian further ported the [Sargon](/Sargon "Sargon") [Z80](/Z80 "Z80") [assembly](/Assembly "Assembly") program by [Kathe](/Kathe_Spracklen "Kathe Spracklen") and [Dan Spracklen](/Dan_Spracklen "Dan Spracklen") [[2]](#cite_note-2) to run under the [CP/M](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CP/M) [operating system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operating_system), and [Harm Geert Muller's](/Harm_Geert_Muller "Harm Geert Muller") [Micro-Max](/Micro-Max "Micro-Max") for an [Atmel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmel)-[ATmega88](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATmega88) [Microcontroller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microcontroller) [[3]](#cite_note-3) using [WinAVR GCC](http://winavr.sourceforge.net/), deployed for a self-made [chess computer](/Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") *SHAH* [[4]](#cite_note-4) and the *ATM18 mini chess computer* [[5]](#cite_note-5) from the electronics magazine [Elektor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elektor).
