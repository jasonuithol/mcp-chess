# John F. White

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/John_F._White

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [People](/People "People") \* John F. White**

**John F. White**,  
a British multiple author [[1]](#cite_note-1), computer consultant and programmer. Fascinated by the later [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire), especially [Aurelian's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aurelian) achievements, he researched and wrote his own account [[2]](#cite_note-2). He is co-programmer of the popular computer Latin translator *Blitz Latin* [[3]](#cite_note-3) and programmer of [Frankfurt Goethe University's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goethe_University_Frankfurt) inscription search engine [[4]](#cite_note-4). He lives in [Wokingham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wokingham), [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England) [[5]](#cite_note-5).

# Querg

As former chess programmer John F. White wrote a series of chess programs in the 80s, called by the generic name [Querg](/Querg "Querg"), all implemented in [6502](/6502 "6502") [assembly](/Assembly "Assembly") to run on an 64K [Atari 130XE](/Atari_8-bit "Atari 8-bit") [[6]](#cite_note-6). In his second [ICCA Journal](/ICGA_Journal#13_1 "ICGA Journal") article he describes how to store and retrieve moves of an [opening book](/Opening_Book "Opening Book") [[7]](#cite_note-7).
