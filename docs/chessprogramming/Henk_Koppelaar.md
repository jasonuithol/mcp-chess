**Hendrik (Henk) Koppelaar**,  
a Dutch physicist, mathematician, computer scientist, [AI](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") researcher and professor emeritus in CS and [knowledge engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_engineering) at [Delft University of Technology](/Delft_University_of_Technology "Delft University of Technology"),
and before, assistant professor in [decision support systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_support_system) at Delft University of Technology, head of [data processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_processing) at [Tilburg University](/Tilburg_University "Tilburg University"), and assistant professor at [Utrecht University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utrecht_University) in [statistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistics) and [mathematical modeling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_model)
[[2]](#cite_note-2).
He holds a Ph.D. in [artificial intelligence](/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") modeling techniques applied to [social sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_science) in 1984 from [University of Amsterdam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Amsterdam)
[[3]](#cite_note-3),
and his research interests in AI include [knowledge based systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge-based_systems) [[4]](#cite_note-4),
[expert systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expert_system) and [computational linguistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_linguistics).
In the 70s, along with [Barend Swets](/Barend_Swets "Barend Swets"), he worked and published on computer chess and artificial intelligence
[[5]](#cite_note-5),
and more recently, along with [Alex B. Meijer](/index.php?title=Alex_B._Meijer&action=edit&redlink=1 "Alex B. Meijer (page does not exist)") on [learning](/Learning "Learning") and [planning](/Planning "Planning") in [Go](/Go "Go")
[[6]](#cite_note-6).
