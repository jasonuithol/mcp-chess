Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/BELCT_2001

The **First Berliner Emanuel-Lasker Computerchess-Tournament** (BELCT) took place at the [Free University of Berlin](/Free_University_of_Berlin "Free University of Berlin"), [Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berlin), from September 29 until October 3, [2001](/Timeline#2001 "Timeline"). The Tournament was the idea of the "Berlin Computer Chess Friends", [Ingo Bauer](/Ingo_Bauer "Ingo Bauer"), [Peter Berger](/Peter_Berger "Peter Berger"), [Thorsten Czub](/Thorsten_Czub "Thorsten Czub"), [Stefan Knappe](/Stefan_Knappe "Stefan Knappe"), [Heiner Marxen](/Heiner_Marxen "Heiner Marxen") and [Volker Pittlik](/index.php?title=Volker_Pittlik&action=edit&redlink=1 "Volker Pittlik (page does not exist)"), and was devoted to [Emanuel Lasker](/Mathematician#EmanuelLasker "Mathematician"), supported by the *Emanuel Lasker Society* [[2]](#cite_note-2). The hardware was kindly provided by [AMD](/AMD "AMD") Germany. As a kind of opening ceremony, professor [Raúl Rojas](/Ra%C3%BAl_Rojas "Raúl Rojas") from the Free University and host of the tournament, hold an exclusive lecture for all participants about [Konrad Zuse's](/Konrad_Zuse "Konrad Zuse") [Plankalkül](/Konrad_Zuse#Plankalkuel "Konrad Zuse"), including some chess routines written in it. The First BELCT was played with ten participants, seven round swiss, time-control 90 minutes per side and game.
