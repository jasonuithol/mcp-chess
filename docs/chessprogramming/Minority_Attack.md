| **Minority attack**,  a method of play typical in positions where white has pawns on a2, b2 and d4, whereas black has pawns on a7, b7, c6 and d5, the so-called [Carlsbad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlsbad_1923_chess_tournament) Pawn Structure [[1]](#cite_note-1). Despite the fact that black nominally has a [pawn majority](/Pawn_Majority "Pawn Majority") on the queenside, white [attacks](/Attacks "Attacks") by moving the b-pawn forward and organizing piece pressure along the c-file. Then either white plays b5xc6, weakening black c-pawn, or black plays c6xb5, opening the c-file and [isolating](/Isolated_Pawn "Isolated Pawn") the d-pawn. | |  | | --- | |                                                                                 ♟♟   ♟♟♟   ♟         ♟        ♙         ♙    ♙♙   ♙♙♙ | |
