Roberto Waldteufel [[1]](#cite_note-1)

**Roberto Waldteufel**, (born 13 Dec 1962)  
a French/British [[2]](#cite_note-2) computer games programmer born in Spain [[3]](#cite_note-3), living on the [Island of Jura, Scotland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jura,_Scotland). Roberto is author of the [Checkers](/Checkers "Checkers") program *Wyllie* [[4]](#cite_note-4), and the chess program [Rabbit](/Rabbit "Rabbit") [[5]](#cite_note-5). Roberto Waldteufel seemed the first one, who mentioned the optimization trick in 1998 to dense the [Rotated Bitboards](/Rotated_Bitboards "Rotated Bitboards") tables, masking off the redundant [outer occupancies](/First_Rank_Attacks#TheOuterSquares "First Rank Attacks") [[6]](#cite_note-6).
