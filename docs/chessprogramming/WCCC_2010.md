The **Eighteenth World Computer Chess Championship** took place from September 24 to October 1, [2010](/Timeline#2010 "Timeline"), at Shiinoki Cultural Complex [[2]](#cite_note-2), [Kanazawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanazawa,_Ishikawa), [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan), in conjunction with the [First World Chess Software Championship](/WCSC_2010 "WCSC 2010"), the [15th Computer Olympiad](/15th_Computer_Olympiad "15th Computer Olympiad") and the [7th Computer and Games](/CG_2010 "CG 2010") conference. All events were hosted by [Japan Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (JAIST)](/JAIST "JAIST") and organized by the [ICGA](/ICGA "ICGA") [[3]](#cite_note-3) . After the [disqualification](/World_Computer_Chess_Championship#RybkaDisqualification "World Computer Chess Championship") of [Rybka](/Rybka "Rybka") in June 2011, [Rondo](/Rondo "Rondo") and [Thinker](/Thinker "Thinker") are World Computer Chess Champions 2010 [[4]](#cite_note-4) .

# Time control

The time control for the WCCC will be such as to aim for a 4-hour playing session, namely all moves in 1 hour 45 minutes plus an increment of 15 seconds per move [[11]](#cite_note-11) .
