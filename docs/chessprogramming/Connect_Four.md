**Connect Four**, (Connect-Four, Connect 4, The Captain's Mistress, Four in a Row, Four in a Line)  
a [tic-tac-toe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tic-tac-toe) like game in which two players drop discs into a 7x6 board. The first player to get four in a row, either vertically, horizontally, or diagonally, wins. The game was known as *The Captain's Mistress*, released in its current form by [Milton Bradley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milton_Bradley_Company) in 1974 (Milton Bradley was acquired by [Hasbro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hasbro) in 1984). Connect Four was first (weakly) [solved](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solved_game) by [James D. Allen](/index.php?title=James_D._Allen&action=edit&redlink=1 "James D. Allen (page does not exist)") as announced in a [rec.games.programmer](/Computer_Chess_Forums "Computer Chess Forums") posting on October 01, 1988 [[2]](#cite_note-2), and only 15 days later, [Victor Allis](/Victor_Allis "Victor Allis") announced his independently discovered solution [[3]](#cite_note-3), described in his thesis [[4]](#cite_note-4).
