# SSE

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/SSE

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Hardware](/Hardware "Hardware") \* [x86](/X86 "X86") \* SSE**

**SSE** (Streaming [SIMD](/SIMD_and_SWAR_Techniques "SIMD and SWAR Techniques") Extensions),   
were introduced by [Intel](/Intel "Intel") in 1999 in their [Pentium III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pentium_III) series of processors. SSE provides an [instruction set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instruction_set) on eight 128-bit xmm registers xmm0..xmm7, with [x86-64](/X86-64 "X86-64") expanded to 16 registers xmm0..xmm15, keeping [vectors](/Array "Array") of four [floats](/Float "Float"). Further, as a reply to [AMD's](/AMD "AMD") [3DNow!](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3DNow!), SSE introduced a few integer instructions extending the [MMX](/MMX "MMX") instruction set.

# SSE2

The [SSE2](/SSE2 "SSE2") instruction set as major enhancement to SSE, offers a more [orthogonal set of instructions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthogonal_instruction_set) with [vectors](/Array "Array") of four [floats](/Float "Float") or two [doubles](/Double "Double"), as well as integer vectors of 16 [bytes](/Byte "Byte"), eight [words](/Word "Word"), four [double words](/Double_Word "Double Word") or two [quad words](/Quad_Word "Quad Word").
