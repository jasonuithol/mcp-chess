**Albert Lindsey Zobrist**,  
an American computer scientist, games researcher, and inventor of the famous [Zobrist Hashing](/Zobrist_Hashing "Zobrist Hashing"), published in 1970 [[1]](#cite_note-1). He is further author of the first [Go program](/Go "Go") in 1968 as part of his Ph.D. Thesis on [pattern recognition](/Pattern_Recognition "Pattern Recognition") at the Computer Science Department of the [University of Wisconsin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Wisconsin-Madison) [[2]](#cite_note-2) [[3]](#cite_note-3).

# Computer Chess

While affiliated with the [University of Southern California](/University_of_Southern_California "University of Southern California") and the [Jet Propulsion Laboratory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jet_Propulsion_Laboratory), Zobrist researched on computer chess, and was along with [Frederic Roy Carlson](/Frederic_Roy_Carlson "Frederic Roy Carlson") and [Charles Kalme](/Charles_Kalme "Charles Kalme") co-author of the chess programs [USC CP](/USC_CP "USC CP") [[4]](#cite_note-4) and [Tyro](/Tyro "Tyro"), participating at five [ACM North American Computer Chess Championships](/ACM_North_American_Computer_Chess_Championship "ACM North American Computer Chess Championship"), [ACM 1972](/ACM_1972 "ACM 1972") and [ACM 1973](/ACM_1973 "ACM 1973") with [USC CP](/USC_CP "USC CP") and [ACM 1974](/ACM_1974 "ACM 1974"), [ACM 1975](/ACM_1975 "ACM 1975") and [ACM 1977](/ACM_1977 "ACM 1977") with [Tyro](/Tyro "Tyro") [[5]](#cite_note-5).
