**Frank Quisinsky**,  
a German computer chess aficionado, journalist and early [WinBoard](/WinBoard "WinBoard") promoter. Along with [Volker Pittlik](/index.php?title=Volker_Pittlik&action=edit&redlink=1 "Volker Pittlik (page does not exist)"), Frank founded the [Winboard Forum](/Computer_Chess_Forums "Computer Chess Forums") in 1999 [[2]](#cite_note-2), and hosts his own computer chess site *Frank's Chess Page* [[3]](#cite_note-3) with a temporary break in 2007, and maintains his own [FCT](/FCT "FCT") [rating list](/Engine_Rating_Lists "Engine Rating Lists") since 2014 [[4]](#cite_note-4). He contributed to the computer chess column of the German chess magazine *Schachwelt* [[5]](#cite_note-5) with interviews and program reports. Notably, Frank was initiator of the [Arena](/Arena "Arena") Chess GUI development realized by [Martin Blume](/Martin_Blume "Martin Blume") [[6]](#cite_note-6). In 2004, he established the Gladiator-Shop as an attempt to commercialize engines such as [Ruffian](/Ruffian "Ruffian") [[7]](#cite_note-7), [Patriot](/Patriot "Patriot") [[8]](#cite_note-8) and [Ktulu](/Ktulu "Ktulu") [[9]](#cite_note-9) bundled with Arena, to provide a small income for their developers and to support further development. The project was abandoned after suspicions that the new Patriot 2.0 was based on [Fruit](/Fruit "Fruit") became substantial in May 2005 [[10]](#cite_note-10) [[11]](#cite_note-11). By August 2005, Frank Quisinsky went out of the Arena project.
