The **Second World Microcomputer Chess Championship** took place from September 21 to 28, [1981](/Timeline#1981 "Timeline"), *Salon Timmendorf* in the [Maritim Hotel](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritim_Travem%C3%BCnde) [[2]](#cite_note-2) , [Travemünde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travem%C3%BCnde), [West Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Germany), hosted and sponsored by the [Horten AG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horten_AG), a German department store chain and primary German [Novag](/Novag "Novag") distributor at that time [[3]](#cite_note-3) . The two last playoff rounds took place in [Hamburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamburg).
The tournament organizers were [Gerhard Piel](/index.php?title=Gerhard_Piel&action=edit&redlink=1 "Gerhard Piel (page does not exist)"), an architect and collector of computer chess machines, [Frederic Friedel](/Frederic_Friedel "Frederic Friedel"), a science journalist and [Dieter Steinwender](/Dieter_Steinwender "Dieter Steinwender"), a computer scientist. Tournament director was [Horst Metzing](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schacholympiade_1986#Luzern_1982) [[4]](#cite_note-4) of the [German Chess Federation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Chess_Federation). The [ICCA](/ICCA "ICCA") along with the [FIDE](/FIDE "FIDE") sanctioned the tournament as a world championship, ICCA observers were [Ben Mittman](/Ben_Mittman "Ben Mittman") and [Ken Thompson](/Ken_Thompson "Ken Thompson").

# Commercial Controversy

The published rules provided for participation in an open and commercial category. The organizers had hoped that by sponsoring a purely commercial section, they could provide the computer chess buying public with a clear indication of which is the strongest commercially available chess machine. In doing this, however, they wanted to avoid what had happened after previous microcomputer chess tournaments when misleading advertising claims were made by manufacturers or by their representatives. The lack of a precise definition for the commercial group became a source for a great deal of controversy, and soured many of the participants, the organizers, and the ICCA observers on this type of tournament [[11]](#cite_note-11) . The requirement for the manufacturers section for commercially available chess computers, forced [Hegener & Glaser](/Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser") to withdraw their intended entry [Mephisto II](/Mephisto_(H) "Mephisto (H)") since commercial production would not begin until November, and Hegener & Glaser chose not to enter in the open group as well. Finally, the [Applied Concepts](/Applied_Concepts "Applied Concepts") personal withdrew their programs after some rounds from both sections of the tournament, due to a bug in their [Capablanca](/Morphy#Capablanca "Morphy") module of the [Great Game Machine](/Great_Game_Machine "Great Game Machine") [[12]](#cite_note-12) .
