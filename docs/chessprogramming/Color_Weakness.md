# Color Weakness

Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Color_Weakness

**[Home](/Main_Page "Main Page") \* [Evaluation](/Evaluation "Evaluation") \* [of Pieces](/Evaluation_of_Pieces "Evaluation of Pieces") \* Color Weakness**

**Color weakness** denotes a situation in which one of the players does not have sufficient [control over the squares](/Square_Control "Square Control") of a certain [color](/Color "Color"). This is usually caused by the lack of a [bishop](/Bishop "Bishop") of that color, coupled with the unfortunate [pawn structure](/Pawn_Structure "Pawn Structure"). In such situations, the opponent may use squares of that color to penetrate the opponent's position. [Hans Kmoch](/Hans_Kmoch "Hans Kmoch") defines insufficient occupation of the dark respectively light squares by pawns and bishop as black poverty or *Melanpenie* respectively white poverty or *[Leukopenie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leukopenia)* [[1]](#cite_note-1).

# Examples

Typical examples of a color weakness are:

- positions after the exchange of a [fianchettoed](/Fianchetto "Fianchetto") bishop (this is especially painful when it was a defender of the castled king)
- black exchanging a dark squared bishop in the [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Defence), the [Slav](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slav_Defense) or the [Caro-Kann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caro-Kann) defences
- white exchanging a light-squared bishop in the [Queen's Gambit Declined](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen%27s_Gambit_Declined) or in the [Colle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colle_System)
- white exchanging a dark-squared bishop in the [King's Indian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King%27s_Indian_Defence), most notably in [Sämisch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King%27s_Indian_Defence#S.C3.A4misch_Variation)
