Source: https://www.chessprogramming.org/Chess960CWC_2009

**[< Prev](/Chess960CWC_2008 "Chess960CWC 2008")**

The **Fifth Livingston** [[1]](#cite_note-1) [Chess960](/Chess960 "Chess960") **Computer World Championship** was held within the scope of the [Chess Classic](http://www.chesstigers.de/ccm9.php?lang=1) [Mainz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainz), Germany, July 29-31, [2009](/Timeline#2009 "Timeline"), last time organized by [Hans-Walter Schmitt](/index.php?title=Hans-Walter_Schmitt&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hans-Walter Schmitt (page does not exist)") and his Chess Tigers [[2]](#cite_note-2). The two finalists from [2008](/Chess960CWC_2008 "Chess960CWC 2008"), [Rybka](/Rybka "Rybka") and [Shredder](/Shredder "Shredder") were challenged by two qualifiers from an online tournament held at [Internet Chess Club](/index.php?title=Internet_Chess_Club&action=edit&redlink=1 "Internet Chess Club (page does not exist)") (ICC), [Deep Sjeng](/Deep_Sjeng "Deep Sjeng") and [Ikarus](/Ikarus "Ikarus") [[3]](#cite_note-3). [Hans Secelle](/Hans_Secelle "Hans Secelle") served as arbiter, and [Eric van Reem](/Eric_van_Reem "Eric van Reem") as Tournament director.
